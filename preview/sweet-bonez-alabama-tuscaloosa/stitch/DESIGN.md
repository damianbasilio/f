I have designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Sweet Bonez Alabama**. 

### Design Decisions:
*   **Visual Identity**: I moved away from common BBQ tropes (checkered tablecloths, cartoon pigs) and instead created a "Pitmaster Editorial" style. The design uses a deep charcoal and bone-white palette with a single ember-orange accent to evoke the intensity and craft of the pit.
*   **Typography**: I paired a high-contrast, woodblock-inspired serif for headings with a clean, wide-set sans-serif for body text, creating a hierarchy that feels both historical and premium.
*   **Layout**: The asymmetric grid and generous use of whitespace give the site a deliberate, gallery-like feel. Thin vertical rules and subtle grainy textures are used as repeating visual motifs to ground the design in the "grit" of Alabama BBQ tradition.
*   **Functional Elements**:
    *   **The Daily Smoke**: A sophisticated offerings section that prioritizes the craft over a generic menu grid.
    *   **Credibility Bar**: A refined stats bar highlighting the brand's Tuscaloosa roots and commitment to hickory smoking.
    *   **Visit & Contact**: A clean, intentional layout for the map placeholder and a custom-styled contact form to maintain the brand's high-end feel.

The result is a production-ready desktop experience that treats Sweet Bonez as a premium local legacy rather than just another smokehouse.

## Brand & Style

The visual identity of the design system centers on an "Elevated Rustic" aesthetic, bridging the gap between the raw, primal energy of a Southern BBQ pit and the sophisticated restraint of high-end editorial design. It is designed to evoke the sensory experience of Tuscaloosa—charcoal, slow-cooked wood, and artisanal craft—without relying on clichés.

The design style is a hybrid of **Minimalism** and **Refined Brutalism**. It utilizes expansive "Bone White" space to allow the "Pitmaster Black" elements to command authority. The atmosphere is quiet but intense, prioritizing high-quality food photography and stark typographic hierarchies over decorative fluff. The user journey should feel like flipping through a premium lithographed cookbook: deliberate, textured, and prestigious.

## Colors

The palette is rooted in the "Smoke & Bone" philosophy. **Pitmaster Black** serves as the primary canvas, creating a dark, immersive environment that mimics the interior of a BBQ smoker. **Bone White** provides the essential editorial contrast for legibility and high-end feel.

**Hickory Ember** is used exclusively as a high-intent accent—reserved for "Order" actions, heat levels, or highlighting specific price points. **Smoke Grey** acts as a structural color for borders, rules, and secondary information, softening the transition between the deep black and stark white. The default state is a dark mode that emphasizes depth and texture.

## Typography

Typography is the primary vehicle for the brand’s "elevated" narrative. 

**Headings** utilize a high-contrast serif, set with tight tracking to mimic vintage woodblock posters found in historic Southern markets. These should be treated as hero elements, often appearing in "Bone White" against "Pitmaster Black."

**Body Text** uses a wide-set, geometric sans-serif. The generous letter spacing is crucial for maintaining an editorial, airy feel amidst the dark color palette. 

**Labels** and micro-copy are always set in uppercase with significant tracking (15%), serving as functional markers that contrast against the fluid elegance of the headlines.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system employs an **Asymmetric Grid** to avoid the predictable "blocky" nature of standard food apps. While built on a 12-column foundation, content should often be offset—for example, a headline spanning 8 columns on the left, with body text occupying the final 4 columns on the right.

Verticality is reinforced by **Thin Vertical Rules** (1px Smoke Grey lines) that separate columns or sections, mimicking the slats of a smokehouse. 

Spacing is intentionally extreme: 
- **High-density** in menu lists to create a sense of abundance and "pitmaster expertise."
- **Generous whitespace** (Section Gaps) between narrative blocks to maintain the premium editorial feel.

## Elevation & Depth

In keeping with the "Rustic" theme, this design system rejects soft, ambient shadows. Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Grainy Textures**:

- **Layer 0 (Base):** Pitmaster Black with a subtle 5% grain overlay to simulate ash or carbon.
- **Layer 1 (Containers):** Smoke Grey surfaces used for cards or secondary modules.
- **Layer 2 (Accents):** Bone White surfaces for high-contrast callouts.

Instead of elevation via shadow, use 1px solid borders in "Smoke Grey" or "Bone White" to define boundaries. Overlapping elements (e.g., an image partially covering a text block) should be used to create physical depth without using drop shadows.

## Shapes

The shape language is **Sharp (0px)**. All containers, buttons, images, and input fields must feature hard 90-degree corners. This reinforces the "woodblock" and "pit" aesthetic—evoking heavy cutting boards, metal smokers, and traditional offset printing. Rounded corners are strictly prohibited as they dilute the masculine, authoritative nature of the BBQ brand.

## Components

### Buttons
Buttons are strictly rectangular. The "Primary Order" button is "Hickory Ember" with "Pitmaster Black" text (Label-caps). Secondary buttons are "Bone White" outlines. Hover states should involve a solid color fill transition with no animation easing, keeping the interaction feeling "mechanical."

### Menu Lists
Menu items should be presented with high density. Title (Headline-md) and Price (Label-caps) should be on the same line, connected by a dotted or solid "Smoke Grey" leader line. Description text (Body-md) sits immediately below in "Smoke Grey."

### Cards
Cards do not use shadows. They are defined by a 1px "Smoke Grey" border. When featured, cards may use a "Pitmaster Black" background that is slightly lighter than the global background to create a "nested" look.

### Thin Rules
Horizontal and vertical rules (1px) are used to cordone off sections. They should never span the full width of the container, often stopping 24px short of the edge to maintain the asymmetric, editorial feel.

### Input Fields
Inputs are simple 1px "Bone White" bottom-borders only. Placeholders should be set in "Smoke Grey" using the Label-caps style.

### Grain Overlay
A global, non-scrolling SVG grain texture at 3-5% opacity should be applied to the entire UI to give the digital surface a tactile, paper-like or soot-like quality.
