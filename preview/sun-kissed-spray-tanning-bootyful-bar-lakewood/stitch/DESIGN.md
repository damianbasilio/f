## Brand & Style
The design system embodies "Golden Hour Chic"—a premium, editorial aesthetic that balances the warmth of a Mediterranean sunset with the sophistication of a high-end fashion magazine. It targets an upscale clientele seeking a luxurious self-care experience. 

The visual style is **Modern Editorial**, characterized by:
- **Asymmetrical Balance:** Breaking the standard grid to create a sense of movement and curated art direction.
- **Intentional Whitespace:** Using the "Creamy Parchment" background as a luxury element itself, allowing content to breathe.
- **Layered Textures:** Subtle use of organic, soft-focus shapes and sun-ray motifs to create depth without clutter.
- **Sophisticated Contrast:** Pairing high-contrast, elegant serifs with utilitarian sans-serifs for a contemporary edge.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in the warmth of "Deep Terracotta" and "Shimmering Gold," providing a sun-kissed glow across the interface.

- **Primary (Deep Terracotta):** Used for key brand moments, primary CTAs, and structural accents. It evokes earthiness and sophisticated tanning.
- **Accent (Shimmering Gold):** Used sparingly for highlights, icons, and decorative motifs to imply luxury and high-end service.
- **Background (Creamy Parchment):** The foundation of the design system. It is softer than pure white, reducing eye strain and providing a premium, paper-like feel.
- **Text (Espresso Black):** Provides sharp, authoritative legibility for headlines and long-form body copy.

## Typography
The typography system relies on a high-contrast pairing to achieve an editorial look.

- **Headlines:** Use **Bodoni Moda** for all display and headline roles. This typeface provides the high-fashion, high-contrast serif look required for a luxury brand. Increase letter-spacing on display sizes to enhance the premium feel.
- **Body & Labels:** Use **Montserrat** for all functional text. Its geometric clarity balances the decorative nature of the serif headlines.
- **Styling Note:** Use "Label-Caps" for overlines or small category descriptors to add a sense of meticulous organization.

## Layout & Spacing
This design system uses a **Fluid Editorial Grid** that eschews standard symmetry for a more dynamic composition.

- **Grid Model:** A 12-column grid on desktop with wide 32px gutters. Elements should frequently "break" the grid—for example, an image might span columns 2 through 7, while its descriptive text starts on column 8 but overlaps the image slightly.
- **Vertical Rhythm:** Utilize large "Stack" units (80px–120px) between sections to create a sense of grandeur and focus.
- **Mobile Reflow:** On mobile, transition to a single-column stack but maintain the overlapping visual motif by using negative margins on image-text pairings to keep the "layered" feel.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is created through **Tonal Layering** and **Soft-Focus Shadows** rather than standard material elevations.

- **Organic Overlaps:** Elements like cards or images should overlap one another with low-opacity, wide-spread shadows (`0 20px 40px rgba(26, 26, 26, 0.05)`).
- **Glassmorphism:** Use subtle backdrop blurs (10px–15px) on navigation bars or floating action panels to suggest a "frosted glass" luxury aesthetic.
- **Texture:** Apply a faint grain texture to the Creamy Parchment background to mimic high-end stationery.

## Shapes
The shape language is refined and "Soft" (0.25rem - 0.75rem). While the layout is structured, the edges are gently blunted to appear more organic and approachable.

- **Primary Elements:** Buttons and input fields use a 4px (Soft) radius.
- **Feature Images:** Use asymmetrical rounding (e.g., top-left and bottom-right corners rounded, others sharp) to reinforce the editorial motif.
- **Motifs:** Incorporate "Sun-ray" patterns using thin 0.5pt lines in Shimmering Gold, often placed behind images or as divider elements.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid Deep Terracotta with Espresso Black or White text. They should be wide with significant horizontal padding and uppercase Montserrat labels. Secondary buttons use a Shimmering Gold "ghost" style (outline only).
- **Cards:** Avoid borders. Use the subtle background tint or a very soft shadow to define card areas. Content inside cards should be asymmetrically aligned.
- **Inputs:** Minimalist bottom-border only inputs for forms, using the Espresso Black for the line and Montserrat for the placeholder text.
- **Chips/Labels:** Small, pill-shaped labels in Shimmering Gold with a 10% opacity background for indicating service categories (e.g., "Full Body," "Sculpting").
- **Interactive Layers:** Use "Floating" booking bars that stick to the bottom of the viewport with a frosted parchment effect.
