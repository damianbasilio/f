I've designed and built a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Holycow Chilean Sandwiches**. 

This isn't a template; it's a unique digital identity that pairs bold Chilean tradition with modern, high-contrast design.

### Design Highlights:
*   **Visual Motif**: I introduced a "Diagonal Slash" motif, inspired by Chilean flag geometry and grill sear marks, used for section transitions and as a bold graphic element.
*   **Typography**: A high-contrast pairing of a heavy flared serif for headings (evoking the intensity of wood-fired cooking) and a clean, wide sans-serif for body copy.
*   **Credibility Ticker**: A high-energy, scrolling text marquee for the "Credibility" section to keep the energy high and the brand message moving.
*   **Editorial Layout**: I opted for an asymmetrical, "intentional density" layout for the Offerings section to make each sandwich feel like a signature masterpiece rather than just a menu item.
*   **Palette**: A warm, smoky palette of **Deep Ember Red**, **Charred Black**, and **Mustard Gold** set against **Parchment** backgrounds to create a premium, tactile feel.

The site includes the requested map placeholder, a functional mobile navigation, and a dark-mode inquiry form. It's fully responsive and production-ready.

## Brand & Style
The design system embodies "Bold Chilean Heritage meets Modern Editorial." It balances the raw, primal energy of wood-fired cooking with the sophisticated restraint of a high-end culinary journal. The aesthetic is tactile and intense, favoring high-contrast compositions and dramatic whitespace.

The visual direction draws from **Modern Minimalism** and **Editorial Design**, utilizing large-scale typography and a rigid grid to anchor "messy" organic textures. A signature diagonal 'slash' motif—inspired by both the Chilean flag and grill sear marks—is used as a structural element for image masks and section dividers, creating a sense of forward momentum and aggression.

**Emotional Response:**
- **Warmth:** Evoked through parchment tones and ember-hued accents.
- **Authority:** Established via heavy serif typography and a dark, grounded base.
- **Purity:** Focused on ingredient macro-photography rather than staged lifestyle shots.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in the heat of the kitchen and the heritage of Chile.

- **Deep Ember Red (#9B2226):** The heart of the brand. Used for primary actions, headlines, and key branding moments. It represents the heat of the coals.
- **Charred Black (#121212):** Used for deep backgrounds and primary text. It provides the "char" that makes the other colors pop.
- **Warm Parchment (#F9F5F1):** The primary surface color. It softens the high-contrast pairing and provides a tactile, paper-like quality to the UI.
- **Mustard Gold (#EE9B00):** An accent reserved for highlights, ratings, and secondary CTAs. It adds a vibrant, "zingy" contrast to the deeper red.

**Color Application:**
Invert the palette for "Dark Mode" sections where Charred Black becomes the surface and Warm Parchment becomes the primary text color to simulate a nighttime grill atmosphere.

## Typography
The typography strategy relies on a tension between the expressive, flared characteristics of the headlines and the technical, clean nature of the body and labels.

- **Headlines:** Use **Bricolage Grotesque**. Its unique, slightly quirky terminals and high-impact weights evoke a modern take on traditional woodblock printing. Use tight leading for a "packed" sandwich feel.
- **Body:** Use **Hanken Grotesk**. Its wide apertures and clean geometry provide a neutral, legible counterpoint to the aggressive headlines.
- **Technical Labels:** Use **Space Mono** for prices, nutritional data, and metadata. This monospaced font adds a modern, "butcher-shop receipt" aesthetic that feels authentic and transparent.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fixed Grid** system with expansive margins to maintain an editorial feel. 

- **The Slash:** A 15-degree diagonal tilt is applied to major layout transitions. Background containers should bleed off-edge following this angle.
- **Desktop:** A 12-column grid with generous 64px outer margins. Content is often offset, leaving empty columns to allow for macro photography of ingredients (smoke, dripping sauces) to occupy the whitespace.
- **Mobile:** A 4-column grid. The 'slash' motif is used more sparingly here to maintain legibility, primarily in image headers and button shapes.
- **Vertical Rhythm:** Built on an 8px base unit. Use large 128px+ gaps between sections to signify premium "breathing room."

## Elevation & Depth
This design system rejects traditional shadows in favor of **Tonal Layering** and **High-Contrast Outlines**.

- **Flat Depth:** Depth is created by stacking Parchment on top of Ember Red or Charred Black.
- **Borders:** Instead of shadows, use 1px or 2px solid borders in Charred Black or Ember Red to define cards and inputs. This reinforces the "Modern Editorial" look.
- **Tactile Overlays:** Use subtle grain textures on large color fills to mimic the feel of premium paper or cast iron.
- **The "Sear" Effect:** Hover states should feel "hot." Instead of lifting an element with a shadow, change the background color to a vibrant Mustard Gold or increase the saturation of the Ember Red.

## Shapes
The shape language is **Sharp (0)**. 

To maintain the "Bold Chilean Heritage" aesthetic, all buttons, cards, and input fields utilize 90-degree corners. This evokes the precision of a chef's knife and the ruggedness of the grill.

- **The Diagonal Cut:** Where "softness" is needed, use a 45-degree or 15-degree clipped corner (chamfer) rather than a radius. 
- **Image Masks:** Images should frequently use the diagonal 'slash' mask on their top or bottom edges to create a sense of being sliced.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Rectangular, sharp-edged, and high-contrast. The primary button is Ember Red with Parchment text. On hover, it transitions to a Mustard Gold fill.
- **Cards:** Defined by a 1px Charred Black border on a Parchment background. The diagonal 'slash' is used at the top of the card to mask the food photography.
- **Lists:** Menu items should be separated by a solid horizontal line (1px Ember Red). Prices are displayed in Space Mono, positioned to the far right.
- **Input Fields:** Bottom-border only (2px Charred Black) to mimic a high-end order form. Labels sit above in Space Mono (Label-Caps).
- **Chips/Tags:** Small, sharp-edged blocks with Mustard Gold backgrounds and Charred Black text, used for dietary labels (e.g., "SPICY," "TRADITIONAL").
- **Signature Component (The Sear Divider):** A thick, 8px horizontal line that is interrupted by a 15-degree diagonal gap, used to separate major content blocks.
