## Brand & Style
The design system embodies a "Rustic-Modern" aesthetic, bridging the gap between traditional craftsmanship and high-end editorial design. It is built for an audience that values slow living, high-quality ingredients, and the tactile nature of a local bakery.

The visual style leans into **Editorial Minimalism** with **Tactile** accents. It utilizes generous whitespace (the "flour") to let the high-contrast typography and rich product photography breathe. The personality is warm and inviting but maintains a sophisticated, premium edge through the use of hairline rules and asymmetrical compositions that suggest a hand-composed, bespoke experience.

## Colors
The palette is inspired by the organic lifecycle of a pie, from raw ingredients to the golden finish of the oven.

- **Flour & Cream (Base):** The foundation of the UI is `#F9F5F0`, a soft buttermilk off-white that feels warmer and more organic than pure white.
- **Warm Crust (Primary):** A rich terracotta `#C05A3E` used for primary actions and brand emphasis.
- **Berry Fill (Secondary):** A deep, sophisticated plum `#5D2A42` used for secondary elements and depth.
- **Harvest Ochre (Tertiary):** Used sparingly for highlights or seasonal accents to bring out the warmth of the "crust" tones.
- **Ink & Charcoal:** For maximum readability, text is set in a very deep plum-charcoal rather than pure black to maintain the warmth of the palette.

## Typography
Typography is the cornerstone of this design system's editorial feel. 

- **Headlines:** Set in **Bodoni Moda**. The high contrast between thick and thin strokes evokes the elegance of food magazines. It should be used with tight kerning for a high-fashion look.
- **Body & UI:** Set in **DM Sans**. This modern sans-serif provides a clean, readable counterpoint to the decorative headlines. 
- **The "Wide-Space" Effect:** Body text and labels should utilize increased letter spacing (0.02em to 0.1em) to create a sense of airiness and luxury. 
- **Stylistic Flourish:** Use italics for secondary headlines or pull-quotes to lean into the artisanal narrative.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout philosophy is **Asymmetrical & Breathable**. Rather than a rigid grid, the system uses "offset blocks" where imagery and text containers overlap or stagger.

- **The Grid:** A 12-column desktop grid with wide 64px margins to create a focused "center-stage" feel.
- **Hairline Rules:** Use 1px rules in the 'Berry Fill' color (at 20% opacity) to separate sections, mimicking the layout of a luxury cookbook.
- **Asymmetry:** Key elements (like hero images or featured pies) should be placed off-center, often breaking the vertical flow of text to create visual interest and a "hand-placed" feel.
- **Negative Space:** Padding should be generous; never crowd the "Flour" (background).

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Subtle Textures** rather than traditional shadows.

- **Stacking:** Use the 'Cream' background as the base. Content blocks may use a slightly darker 'Ochre' tint or a 'Plum' tint to define hierarchy.
- **Flour Dusting:** Apply subtle, low-opacity grain textures to background containers to mimic the look of dusted flour on a wooden table.
- **Flat Depth:** If depth is required for interactive elements, use 1px solid borders in a slightly darker shade of the element’s color to create a "pressed" look, rather than using blurs.

## Shapes
The shape language is primarily **Soft** and structured.

- **Base Radius:** Use a subtle 0.25rem (4px) radius for buttons and input fields to soften the "modern" edges while maintaining the "editorial" structure.
- **Organic Cuts:** Photography should occasionally use "arch" masks or organic, hand-drawn container shapes to reflect the irregular beauty of a hand-crimped pie crust.
- **The Hairline:** Vertical and horizontal lines are always sharp and 1px wide, providing a precise frame for the more organic content.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use the 'Warm Crust' (Terracotta) fill with white text. They should be rectangular with a very slight soft corner (4px) and use uppercase 'DM Sans' labels with high letter-spacing.
- **Input Fields:** Minimalist design with only a bottom-border hairline. On focus, the hairline transitions from 'Berry Fill' 20% to 'Warm Crust' 100%.
- **Cards:** Cards should not have shadows. Instead, use a 1px 'Berry Fill' (low opacity) border or a subtle 'Buttermilk' fill that is slightly darker than the main background.
- **Chips/Labels:** Small, pill-shaped tags used for "Seasonal" or "Award Winning" markers, utilizing the 'Berry Fill' color with white text.
- **Asymmetrical Image Blocks:** A custom component where an image is paired with a caption that sits 24px lower than the image's top edge, breaking the horizontal alignment.
- **The "Ingredients" List:** Lists should use wide-spaced typography and custom terracotta bullet points that look like tiny hand-drawn dots or crumbs.
