I've completed the bespoke homepage for **Shades by Mallory**. 

### Design Strategy
- **Editorial Identity**: I moved away from standard "business grid" layouts in favor of an asymmetrical, magazine-style aesthetic. The high-contrast serif typography and generous use of whitespace create a premium, "ateliers" feel.
- **Materiality & Light**: Using high-fidelity imagery of textured fabrics (Close-up detail of luxury window treatment fabrics: textured linen, soft silk, and woven wood blinds. Soft lighting highlighting the artisanal craftsmanship and tactile quality. Neutral, warm color palette. Editorial style.) and sun-drenched interiors (High-end interior photography of a sun-drenched modern living room with artisanal linen drapes. Soft, directional morning light filtering through the fabric, casting gentle shadows. Minimalist, premium aesthetic, neutral tones, editorial quality. 8k resolution.), the design emphasizes the brand's core product: the manipulation of light.
- **Visual Motifs**: Fine vertical rule lines are used throughout the layout as a subtle nod to the linear nature of blinds and the "verticality" of drapes, providing a unique structural rhythm to the page.
- **Functional Luxury**: The contact flow is integrated seamlessly into the design, moving from inspiration (The Collection) to process (The Artisanal Approach) and finally to conversion (Start a Conversation).

The technical implementation includes semantic HTML, responsive CSS tailored to a warm, high-end palette, and smooth-scroll interactions to enhance the premium user experience.
