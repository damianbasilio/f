I have designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Nails And Spa By Patricia, LLC**. 

### Design Rationale:
*   **Visual Identity:** Instead of a typical salon template, I created a "Warm Sand & Rose" aesthetic. The palette uses deep terracotta (#8C4A32) and soft blush tones to evoke the sophistication of high-end nail lacquer and skin care.
*   **Editorial Layout:** I employed an asymmetric layout with generous whitespace, overlapping borders, and a strong typographic hierarchy (using a high-contrast serif for headings). This creates a premium, "magazine-style" feel that differentiates the business from local competitors.
*   **Bespoke Sections:** 
    *   **The Menu:** Replaced standard cards with a refined, layered service grid.
    *   **The Lakeland Standard:** A dedicated credibility section emphasizing artisanal precision and local reputation.
    *   **Technical Integrity:** The page is fully responsive, features semantic HTML, and includes the specific required map-slot for the Lakeland location.

The resulting design is unique, professional, and built to make a memorable first impression for Patricia's clients.

## Brand & Style
The design system embodies the high-fashion, bespoke essence of a premium spa experience. The personality is sophisticated, calm, and intentionally curated, moving away from utility toward an editorial, boutique-lifestyle aesthetic. 

The visual style is **Minimalist-Editorial** with **Tactile** layering. It utilizes generous whitespace to create a sense of luxury and "breathing room," akin to a high-end fashion magazine. Key visual motifs include thin, overlapping borders that suggest precision, and organic, "liquid" shapes inspired by nail lacquer pools to break the rigidity of the grid. The emotional response should be one of immediate serenity and an assurance of artisanal quality.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in earthiness and skin-tone elegance. 
- **Deep Terracotta (#8C4A32)**: The primary brand color, used for high-impact accents, call-to-action elements, and sophisticated line work.
- **Soft Blush (#F2D8D5)**: Used for secondary surfaces, hovering states, and subtle depth layers.
- **Warm Sand (#F5F2ED)**: The main background color, providing a softer, more premium feel than pure white.
- **Charcoal (#2D2D2D)**: Used exclusively for typography and fine-line borders to ensure high legibility and a grounded, professional contrast.

Avoid using pure black or stark white to maintain the "organic luxury" feel.

## Typography
The typographic system relies on the high-contrast tension between **Bodoni Moda** and **DM Sans**. 

- **Headlines**: Bodoni Moda provides a classic, high-fashion editorial look. Use larger sizes for display text, ensuring the high-contrast serifs are visible.
- **Body**: DM Sans is used for its airy, modern, and neutral character. It balances the drama of the serif with clean legibility.
- **Labels**: Small-scale labels and "overline" text should use DM Sans in uppercase with increased letter spacing to evoke a premium brand mark feel.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout uses a **Fluid Editorial Grid**. While it follows a 12-column structure on desktop, elements should frequently "break" the grid or overlap with neighboring sections to create an asymmetrical, bespoke feel.

- **Asymmetry**: Offset images and text blocks. For example, an image might span columns 2-7 while the accompanying text begins on column 8 but is vertically centered.
- **Whitespace**: Margin and padding should be generous. Never crowd the content.
- **Overlays**: Use "floating" elements where a thin-bordered box (Charcoal #2D2D2D, 0.5px) partially overlaps a photograph or a Soft Blush color block.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Thin Outlines** rather than heavy shadows.

- **Tiers**: Background (Warm Sand) -> Mid-ground (Soft Blush blocks) -> Foreground (White or Terracotta cards).
- **Outlines**: Use 0.5pt or 1pt borders in Charcoal (#2D2D2D) with 20% opacity for container definition.
- **Layering**: Elements should appear "stacked" like pieces of high-quality stationery. If a shadow is required for a floating action, use a very large, extremely soft (2% opacity) Charcoal shadow that mimics natural ambient light in a spa.

## Shapes
The primary shape language is **Sharp and Architectural**, contrasted with **Organic Accent Shapes**.

- **Structural Elements**: Buttons, inputs, and layout containers use a `0px` radius (Sharp) to maintain a high-fashion, high-contrast look.
- **Lacquer Motifs**: Abstract, blob-like shapes with varying radii are used as background decorative elements (behind images or sections) to mimic the organic flow of nail polish. These should be in Soft Blush or a faint Terracotta.

## Components
- **Buttons**: Square corners. Primary buttons are solid Deep Terracotta with White text. Secondary buttons are transparent with a 1px Charcoal border. All-caps labels with wide tracking.
- **Inputs**: Minimalist single-line inputs with the label positioned above in `label-caps`. Focus state changes the bottom border to Deep Terracotta.
- **Cards**: No shadows. Use thin (0.5px) Charcoal borders or subtle Warm Sand background shifts.
- **Chips/Badges**: Small, sharp-edged rectangles using Soft Blush backgrounds and Charcoal text.
- **Nail Polish Selector**: Custom component using the "liquid" organic shape motif as the selection indicator rather than a standard radio button.
- **Booking Lists**: High whitespace between items, separated by 0.5px Charcoal horizontal rules (hairlines).
