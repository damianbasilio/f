*   **Curated Content**: I've replaced generic placeholder text with brand-specific copy that focuses on the salon's precision, artistic integrity, and local Olathe roots.

## Brand & Style
The design system is built on an **Editorial Minimalism** aesthetic, positioning the salon as a high-end art studio rather than a standard service provider. The brand personality is sophisticated, confident, and avant-garde, targeting a clientele that views nail care as a form of personal curation and self-expression.

The visual narrative is driven by the tension between structured, high-fashion layouts and organic, fluid "brush stroke" elements. The UI should evoke the feeling of a luxury fashion monograph—intentional, quiet, and premium. Key characteristics include:
- **Asymmetric Balance:** Breaking the traditional grid to create a dynamic, gallery-like experience.
- **Large-Scale Imagery:** Hero sections and service categories utilize full-bleed or oversized photography with dramatic lighting.
- **Intentional Whitespace:** Using "Alabaster" not just as a background, but as a structural element to frame content and allow the "Lacquer Red" accents to pop with maximum impact.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in high-contrast "Onyx" and "Alabaster" to create a timeless, editorial foundation. "Lacquer Red" serves as the singular, aggressive point of focus, mimicking the sheen and intensity of fresh nail polish.

- **Primary (Lacquer Red):** Used exclusively for calls to action, active states, and signature decorative brush strokes. It represents the "art" in the artistry.
- **Secondary (Onyx):** Used for primary typography and heavy structural blocks to provide grounding and a sense of luxury.
- **Neutral (Alabaster):** The primary canvas. It should feel expansive and clean, providing a gallery-white backdrop for photography.
- **Functional Grays:** Use low-saturation tints of Onyx for subtle borders and secondary information to maintain the monochromatic sophistication.

## Typography
This design system employs a high-contrast typographic pairing to reinforce the luxury editorial theme.

- **Headlines (Bodoni Moda):** A bold, wide serif with extreme stroke contrast. It should be used at large scales to anchor sections. For "Display" sizes, use tighter letter spacing to create a dense, "Vogue-style" aesthetic.
- **Body & Labels (Hanken Grotesk):** A contemporary, sharp sans-serif that provides a technical, clean counterpoint to the romanticism of the serif. 
- **The "Editorial Quote":** Use italicized Bodoni Moda for pull-quotes or artistic captions to break the rhythm of standard body text.
- **Spacing:** Ensure generous line heights for body text to maintain the airy, premium feel of the layout.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Modified 12-Column Grid** that encourages "grid-breaking." While text is generally aligned to the grid for readability, images and decorative brush strokes should bleed off the edges or overlap grid lines to create a sense of movement.

- **Desktop:** 12 columns with wide 32px gutters. Use large margins (64px+) to create a framed, magazine-page effect.
- **Mobile:** 4 columns with 16px gutters and 24px margins. Typography scales down aggressively to maintain the "big type" look without breaking words awkwardly.
- **Rhythm:** Use a strict 8px baseline grid, but utilize large vertical gaps (e.g., 128px or 160px) between major sections to emphasize the premium nature of the content.

## Elevation & Depth
In this design system, depth is achieved through **Layering and Transparency** rather than traditional shadows.

- **Tonal Layering:** Use "Onyx" surfaces for immersive sections, with "Lacquer Red" elements appearing to float on top.
- **The "Varnish" Effect:** Apply subtle high-gloss blurs behind navigation bars or overlaying images to mimic the texture of top-coat lacquer.
- **Flat Depth:** Elements are tiered using z-index overlaps (e.g., a headline partially overlapping a portrait image) rather than drop shadows. This maintains the 2D editorial feel.
- **Zero-Shadow Policy:** Avoid shadows entirely. If an element needs to feel elevated, use a high-contrast border or a subtle tonal shift in the background color.

## Shapes
The shape language is a study in contrasts: **Rigid Architecture vs. Organic Fluidity.**

- **Structural Elements:** Buttons, cards, and image containers use **Sharp (0px)** corners to reflect high-fashion precision and architectural rigor.
- **Organic Motifs:** "Brush stroke" textures are used as background masks or decorative overlays. These should feel hand-painted, irregular, and fluid, mimicking the stroke of a nail polish brush.
- **Containers:** Image containers should favor vertical orientations (3:4 or 2:3 aspect ratios) to mimic magazine layouts.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are sharp-edged, solid "Onyx" or "Lacquer Red" with "label-caps" typography. Secondary buttons use a fine 1px border with no fill.
- **Inputs:** Minimalist bottom-border only. Labels use "label-caps" and sit above the field. Errors are highlighted in "Lacquer Red."
- **Cards:** No borders or shadows. Cards rely on generous padding and distinct background tones (Alabaster vs. Accent Soft) to separate content.
- **The "Artist List":** Service menus should be styled like a table of contents in a book—clean lines, dot leaders (optional), and Bodoni Moda for service names.
- **Brush-Stroke Accents:** A custom component that randomly rotates a SVG "lacquer swipe" behind key images or headlines to ensure the "Modern Artistry" theme is consistently represented.
- **Sticky Navigation:** A minimal, high-contrast bar that remains at the top, featuring the salon's signature and a "Book Now" CTA in Lacquer Red.
