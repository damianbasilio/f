## Brand & Style

The design system is built on a foundation of "Editorial Playfulness," targeting premium pet owners who value high-quality care with a modern, high-energy edge. The personality is confident and energetic yet approachable, avoiding the clinical coldness of traditional grooming services.

The style is a hybrid of **Minimalism** and **High-Contrast Boldness**. It utilizes generous whitespace to maintain a premium feel while employing aggressive, thick offset borders and asymmetrical layouts to inject energy. The emotional response should be one of trust and excitement—like a high-end fashion magazine meets a vibrant community hub. Physical metaphors like "bubbles" and "suds" are abstracted into clean, geometric pill shapes and circular motifs.

## Colors

The palette, "Bubble Bath & Bark," uses high-contrast pairings to drive action and define structure. 

- **Deep Navy (#1A2B3C):** Used for primary text, thick borders, and grounding structural elements. It provides the "Editorial" weight.
- **Vibrant Teal (#2DD4BF):** The primary brand color, used for key actions, "sudsy" accents, and high-energy highlights.
- **Treat Orange (#FB923C):** The "Pop" color. Reserved for secondary CTAs, alerts, or specific celebratory moments (like a completed grooming session).
- **Soft Suds White (#F8FAFC):** The primary canvas color. It is cool-toned to ensure the Teal and Orange feel vibrant rather than neon.

## Typography

This design system uses a high-contrast typographic pairing. **Bricolage Grotesque** brings a quirky, rounded, and expressive energy to headlines, making the brand feel friendly and distinctive. Its idiosyncratic letterforms (like the 'g' and 'y') serve as a core brand element.

**Hanken Grotesk** is used for body and labels to provide a clean, professional counter-balance. It ensures that service descriptions and booking forms remain highly legible and "spacious." Use tighter line-heights for the heavy headlines to maintain a compact, editorial impact, while keeping body text airy.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system rejects rigid, symmetrical grids in favor of a **dynamic editorial layout**. 

- **Asymmetry:** Content should frequently "break the box," with images or text blocks overlapping their containers.
- **The "Offset" Rule:** Primary containers do not use shadows; instead, they use a 2px Deep Navy border with a solid 4px Teal or Orange offset shadow (hard edge).
- **Whitespace:** Use a minimum of 64px padding between major sections to maintain a premium feel.
- **Mobile Reflow:** On mobile, asymmetrical overlaps should transition into vertical stacks, but maintain the thick "offset" borders to keep the brand identity intact.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth in this design system is created through **layering and hard offsets** rather than soft shadows. 

1.  **Level 0 (Base):** Soft Suds White background.
2.  **Level 1 (Cards/Sections):** White surfaces with a 2px Deep Navy border.
3.  **Level 2 (Active/Floating):** Surfaces with a 2px Deep Navy border and a solid 4px - 8px color block offset (Teal or Orange) positioned to the bottom-right.
4.  **Bubbles:** Circular, semi-transparent Suds White or Teal elements floating in the background or overlapping edges to create a sense of movement.

Avoid all blurs or gradients. Depth is literal and geometric.

## Shapes

The shape language is dominated by **pill shapes and large-scale radii**. This mirrors the "bubbles" and "splashes" of the pet wash theme.

- **Pill Shapes:** Mandatory for buttons, chips, and navigational tabs.
- **Large Radius:** Cards and containers use `rounded-xl` (1.5rem / 24px) to soften the impact of the thick Navy borders.
- **Bubbles:** Use perfect circles for background patterns or as "treat" indicators. No sharp corners should exist in the UI except for the literal 90-degree angles of the hard-offset "shadows."

## Components

- **Buttons:** Large, pill-shaped. Primary buttons are Teal with Navy text and a thick Navy border. On hover, the button shifts 2px to the top-left to "cover" its offset shadow.
- **Cards:** White background, 2px Navy border, 24px corner radius. Use an asymmetrical image placement where the pet photo breaks the top or side boundary of the card.
- **Chips/Badges:** Small pill shapes using Treat Orange for "New" or "Popular" tags, ensuring they pop against the Navy/Teal environment.
- **Inputs:** Suds White background with a bottom-only 2px Navy border. On focus, the border becomes Teal and the label floats.
- **Bubble Lists:** Instead of standard bullet points, use small Teal circles (bubbles) with a white "shine" dot in the top-left corner.
- **Progress Indicators:** Use a "filling tub" metaphor—a wide pill shape that fills with Teal from left to right as a user moves through the booking flow.
