## Brand & Style
The design system embodies "Modern Southern Elegance," a sophisticated fusion of traditional Southern hospitality and contemporary editorial design. It targets a discerning local and visiting clientele in Mobile, AL, who appreciate curated, boutique experiences. 

The aesthetic is heavily rooted in **Minimalism** with **Editorial** flourishes. It prioritizes quiet luxury through generous whitespace, high-contrast typography, and a "less is more" philosophy. The emotional response is one of calm, exclusivity, and warmth—avoiding the clinical feel of modern tech in favor of a textured, lived-in sophistication.

## Colors
The palette is inspired by the natural textures of the Gulf Coast and historic architecture.

- **Deep Magnolia (#2C332D):** A rich, desaturated charcoal-green used for primary text, deep backgrounds, and grounding elements.
- **Alabaster (#F9F8F3):** A warm, textured off-white that serves as the primary canvas, providing a softer, more premium feel than pure white.
- **Copper Leaf (#AD7353):** A refined metallic accent used sparingly for interactive elements, thin rules, and highlights.
- **Dusk Neutral (#6B6D64):** A mid-tone used for secondary text and subtle borders to maintain low-contrast elegance.

## Typography
The typography pairing is the cornerstone of this design system's editorial feel. 

**Libre Caslon Text** provides an authoritative, literary weight for headlines. Use it for storytelling and primary headers. **DM Sans** offers a clean, geometric counterpoint. For secondary text and labels, apply wide letter-spacing (tracking) to the sans-serif to enhance the "boutique" aesthetic. 

Maintain a strict vertical rhythm, using large font sizes for impact and smaller, tracked-out caps for metadata and navigation.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fixed Grid** philosophy with intentional **Asymmetry**. 

- **Desktop:** A 12-column grid with wide 80px margins. Break the grid by offsetting images or text blocks (e.g., an image spanning columns 2-7 with text beginning on column 9).
- **Whitespace:** Use "Section Gaps" (120px+) to separate distinct narratives, allowing the eye to rest.
- **Copper Rules:** Vertical and horizontal 1px rules in 'Copper Leaf' should be used to separate content sections or frame asymmetrical blocks, mimicking high-end stationery.
- **Mobile:** Transition to a single-column flow but maintain the 24px "breathing room" margin and preserve the use of thin rules for structure.

## Elevation & Depth
This design system rejects heavy shadows in favor of **Tonal Layers** and **Low-Contrast Outlines**.

Depth is created by layering Deep Magnolia over Alabaster. When an element needs to feel "elevated" (like a modal or a floating card), use a very faint, 1px border in 'Dusk Neutral' or a subtle 10% opacity 'Deep Magnolia' shadow with a large 40px blur to suggest ambient light rather than a physical drop. 

Avoid "material" style elevations. The "depth" should feel like layers of premium paper.

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0)**. 

To maintain the high-end editorial and architectural feel, all buttons, input fields, images, and containers must have 90-degree corners. This evokes the precision of a printed magazine and the structural elegance of Southern masonry. Rounding is only permitted for specific organic icons if necessary, but never for structural UI components.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use a 'Deep Magnolia' background with 'Alabaster' text, sharp corners, and 16px vertical padding. Secondary buttons are "Ghost" style with a 1px 'Copper Leaf' bottom border (underline) and no side borders.
- **Cards:** Cards should be borderless with an 'Alabaster' or 'Deep Magnolia' fill. Use asymmetrical padding (e.g., more padding on the bottom than the top) to maintain the editorial look.
- **Inputs:** Simple 1px 'Dusk Neutral' bottom border. The label sits above in 'label-caps' style. No background fill unless focused.
- **Chips/Labels:** Small, all-caps text with wide tracking, separated by a thin vertical 'Copper Leaf' rule instead of a container.
- **Lists:** Use 'Copper Leaf' numerals (Libre Caslon) for ordered lists to add a decorative, sophisticated touch.
- **Interactive Accents:** On hover, 'Copper Leaf' elements should subtly shift in opacity or gain a 2px stroke weight to indicate interactivity without breaking the minimalist aesthetic.
