I have designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Remares Towing & Recovery** that establishes a premium, industrial visual identity.

### Design Rationale:
*   **Industrial Editorial Aesthetic**: I moved away from generic local business templates in favor of a high-contrast "blueprint" style. The design uses **Midnight Asphalt** and **Caution Amber** to evoke the feeling of heavy-duty machinery and precision rescue operations.
*   **Typographic Hierarchy**: The site utilizes oversized, bold serif headlines to command authority, paired with monospaced data labels to reinforce the "technical discipline" of recovery services.
*   **Unique Grid Motif**: The "Rescue Manifest" (Services) section uses architectural rule lines and structural grid layouts instead of standard cards, creating a memorable visual motif that repeats throughout the page.
*   **Local Credibility**: The content is grounded in its Campton, NH location, treating the business as a "Local Command" rather than just a service provider.

### Key Features:
*   **Production-Ready Code**: A full semantic HTML structure with integrated CSS custom properties for the brand palette.
*   **Interactive Elements**: A mobile-responsive navigation menu, smooth-scrolling anchors, and a high-fidelity contact form with built-in validation UI.
*   **Strategic Layout**: The page flow transitions from high-impact hero visuals to a dense, data-driven credibility ticker, ensuring a professional first impression.

The empty map slot and all technical constraints (603-988-1501, 109 Black Hall Rd) have been implemented as requested.

## Brand & Style

The design system is built on the concept of "Industrial Sophistication." It bridges the gap between rugged, heavy-duty roadside recovery and the precision of high-end logistics. The target audience includes premium vehicle owners and commercial fleet managers who value reliability over haste.

The visual style is **Editorial Minimalism mixed with Technical Brutalism**. It utilizes expansive white space to denote calm under pressure, contrasted with high-density information blocks that reflect technical expertise. Instead of common towing tropes, the design uses architectural "rule" dividers and subtle grid lines to evoke the feeling of a structural blueprint or a perfectly executed rescue operation. The emotional response should be one of immediate relief, grounded in the presence of an expert.

## Colors

The palette is rooted in the environment of New Hampshire infrastructure. 

*   **Midnight Asphalt (#1A1C1E):** Used for primary surfaces, heavy typography, and structural elements. It provides the "weight" of the brand.
*   **Caution Amber (#FFB800):** A high-visibility accent used sparingly for calls to action, status indicators, and critical highlights. It represents the "Rescue" aspect.
*   **Gravel Grey (#E2E8F0):** Used for background sections and subtle UI dividers to maintain an airy, editorial feel.
*   **Base White (#FFFFFF):** The primary canvas, ensuring the "Editorial" aesthetic remains clean and readable.

## Typography

This design system employs a high-contrast typographic pairing to signal both heritage and precision. 

**Headlines (Libre Caslon Text):** A bold, authoritative serif that feels like a broadsheet newspaper or a premium luxury brand. It should be set with tight leading and slight negative letter-spacing for large displays.

**Body (Hanken Grotesk):** A precision-engineered sans-serif that ensures maximum legibility for service descriptions and technical details.

**Technical Data (Geist):** A monospaced font used for "utility" information like coordinates, truck IDs, and timestamps, reinforcing the blueprint motif.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout follows a **Rigid 12-Column Grid** inspired by architectural drawings. 

*   **Rule Dividers:** Vertical and horizontal 1px lines (Midnight Asphalt at 10-20% opacity) should be used to separate content blocks instead of traditional cards or shadows.
*   **Asymmetric Density:** Group technical information tightly in one column, while leaving the adjacent columns empty to create "editorial breathing room."
*   **Mobile Reflow:** On mobile, the 12-column grid collapses to a single column, but the 1px rule dividers remain to maintain the structural "blueprint" identity.

## Elevation & Depth

This design system rejects traditional shadows in favor of **Tonal Layering and Border-Based Depth**. 

*   **Flat Stacking:** Hierarchy is achieved through color contrast (e.g., a Midnight Asphalt block on a Gravel Grey background).
*   **The "Rule" System:** Instead of elevation, use 1px or 2px solid borders to define containers. 
*   **Active States:** Elements don't "lift"; they change color or gain a heavy 4px inner border of Caution Amber to signal selection.
*   **Inlays:** Use subtle 1px insets to make input fields feel "etched" into the interface rather than floating above it.

## Shapes

To maintain the industrial and architectural aesthetic, the design system utilizes **Sharp (0px)** corners exclusively. This conveys stability, structural integrity, and precision. Avoid all rounded corners on buttons, cards, and input fields. The only exception is for circular icons where strictly necessary for universal recognition (e.g., a phone icon).

## Components

*   **Buttons:** Rectangular with no radius. Primary buttons are Midnight Asphalt with White text. Secondary buttons use a Caution Amber border with no fill. High-priority "Emergency" buttons use a solid Caution Amber fill with Midnight Asphalt text.
*   **Editorial Cards:** Content is contained by 1px rules rather than shadows. The top-left corner of important cards should feature a small "Geist" mono-label (e.g., "UNIT_04" or "LOC_DATA").
*   **Input Fields:** Ghost-style inputs with a bottom-border only (2px Midnight Asphalt). Labels should use the `label-caps` typography style and be placed above the field.
*   **Status Indicators:** Small, sharp-edged squares of Caution Amber to indicate active service or urgent alerts.
*   **Dividers:** Use 1px `tertiary_color_hex` lines. For major section breaks, use a 4px "Industrial Bar" in Midnight Asphalt.
*   **Lists:** Service lists should be styled like a manifest—high-density text, left-aligned, separated by thin horizontal rules.
