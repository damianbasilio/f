I have designed and built a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Cecis Grill House Food Truck**. 

### Design Rationale:
- **Visual Identity**: I moved away from generic food truck templates by adopting a **'Modern Industrial Grill'** aesthetic. This uses a high-contrast palette of **Smoked Onyx** and **Raw Canvas**, paired with bold, high-contrast serif typography (**Playfair Display**) to evoke the feel of a premium food magazine.
- **Layout & Hierarchy**: The layout utilizes an **asymmetrical grid** and **intentional whitespace** to create a memorable, premium first impression. Overlapping typographic elements in the hero and staggered sections in 'The Spread' ensure the site feels designed rather than assembled.
- **Local Credibility**: A dedicated 'Roots' strip anchors the brand in Lexington, KY, while the 'About' section uses a two-column editorial style to tell a narrative-driven story of quality and craftsmanship.
- **Functionality**: The site includes a custom contact form, the required empty map slot for the Lexington location, and a clean, responsive header and footer with direct contact links.

The result is a unique, production-ready landing page that positions Cecis Grill House as a premium local destination.

## Brand & Style
The design system establishes a premium, editorial identity for a high-end mobile culinary experience. The brand personality is "Refined Grit"—a sophisticated intersection of industrial textures and award-winning gastronomy. It targets food enthusiasts who appreciate the craft of a wood-fired grill but demand the aesthetic polish of a boutique restaurant.

The visual style is **Modern Industrial**, characterized by:
- **Editorial Layouts:** High-contrast compositions with generous whitespace and intentional asymmetry.
- **Tactile Minimalism:** Raw, physical textures (charcoal grain, heavy paper stock) paired with razor-sharp vector lines.
- **The "Street" Edge:** A mix of luxury serif elegance and condensed, authoritative typography reminiscent of industrial signage.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in the physical elements of the grill: smoke, fire, and heat.
- **Smoked Onyx (#1A1A1A):** Used for primary text and structural containers to provide weight and authority.
- **Charred Ember (#E63946):** The primary call-to-action color, representing the heat of the grill.
- **Golden Sear (#F4A261):** Used for highlights, secondary buttons, and premium badges.
- **Raw Canvas (#FDFCF8):** The base background color, providing an organic, "heavy paper" editorial feel.
- **Slate Depth (#2B3A42):** Used for subtle backgrounds and deep shadows to add dimension.

## Typography
The typography system uses a tri-font approach to balance luxury with industrial utility. 
- **Display & Accents:** `Bebas Neue` provides a rugged, condensed industrial look for short titles and navigation.
- **Headlines:** `Playfair Display` provides high-contrast serif elegance for editorial impact.
- **Body:** `Source Sans 3` ensures maximum legibility with a clean, neutral presence.
- **Technical Labels:** `Space Mono` is used for pricing, ingredients, and meta-data to reinforce the "industrial" blueprint aesthetic.

## Layout & Spacing
This design system utilizes an **asymmetrical grid** inspired by modern magazine spreads.
- **The 12-Column Grid:** Elements should often break the grid, with images overlapping text containers or spanning 7/12th of the width to create dynamic tension.
- **Spacious Rhythm:** Vertical spacing is aggressive (`section-gap: 120px`) to ensure the content feels premium and curated rather than cluttered.
- **The "Frame" Rule:** Use thin 1px borders (`Smoked Onyx` at 20% opacity) to create internal frames within the layout, mimicking the layout of a technical specification sheet or high-end menu.
- **Mobile:** Transition to a single-column flow with reduced margins (`20px`), but maintain the high-contrast display typography.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and physical metaphors rather than soft shadows.
- **The Paper Stack:** Use `Raw Canvas` as the base, with slightly darker `Slate Depth` or `Smoked Onyx` sections to indicate priority.
- **No Shadows:** Avoid traditional drop shadows. Use hard, 1px borders or "offset blocks" (a solid color block shifted 4px down and right) to create a brutalist sense of depth.
- **Texture Overlays:** Apply a subtle charcoal grain or recycled paper texture (multiply blend mode at 5% opacity) across the entire UI to kill the "digital" flatness.
- **Glass Effects:** Use heavy background blurs on navigation bars only, simulating a frosted glass "chef's pass."

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px)**. 
- All buttons, input fields, cards, and images must use hard 90-degree corners to reflect the industrial, metal-heavy environment of a high-end grill. 
- Use vertical and horizontal 1px lines to separate content, reinforcing the grid-based, architectural feel.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid `Smoked Onyx` with `Raw Canvas` text in `Bebas Neue`. Secondary buttons are "Ghost" style—1px borders with centered text. Active states use `Charred Ember` fill.
- **Cards:** Product and menu cards feature full-bleed photography. Text labels should be placed in high-contrast blocks (e.g., a `Smoked Onyx` box with white `Space Mono` text) overlapping the corner of the image.
- **Input Fields:** Minimalist design with only a bottom border in `Smoked Onyx`. Labels use `label-mono` style.
- **Chips/Badges:** Small, rectangular containers with `label-mono` typography, used for food tags (e.g., "SPICY", "LOCAL").
- **Food Photography:** Images should be high-contrast with deep blacks. Use a "duotone" hover effect that shifts the image toward a warm, amber tint (`Golden Sear`).
- **Interactive Frames:** Use thin frame lines that "grow" or animate when hovering over clickable menu items to emphasize the editorial grid.
