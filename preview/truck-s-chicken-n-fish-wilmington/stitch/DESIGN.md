I've designed a bespoke homepage for **Truck's Chicken 'n Fish** that breaks away from generic restaurant templates. The design, centered around the "Legendary Grit" of Wilmington's Red Cross Street, uses a high-contrast editorial layout to create a premium, local-legend identity.

### Design Rationale:
- **Editorial Typography**: I paired a bold, condensed serif for headlines with a spacious sans-serif, creating a "newsprint" feel that honors the business's deep local roots and heritage.
- **Moody Palette**: The "Cast Iron" and "Red Cross Crimson" palette provides a sophisticated, high-end backdrop that makes the golden textures of the food photography pop.
- **Asymmetrical Layout**: By using overlapping containers and heavy vertical strokes, the site feels designed and intentional, steering clear of standard component grids.
- **Heritage-First Hierarchy**: The 604 Red Cross Street location is woven into the narrative and visual motif of the site, establishing immediate credibility and local pride.

The code is production-ready, featuring semantic HTML, custom CSS properties for the unique palette, and responsive logic for a seamless experience across devices.
