I've designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Gloss n Glow Mobile Auto Spa Omaha**.

### Design Strategy
- **Visual Identity:** I moved away from common service industry templates to create a high-contrast "Midnight Onyx and Copper" aesthetic. This evokes a sense of premium craftsmanship and luxury.
- **Editorial Layout:** The design uses a sophisticated grid with thin copper hairlines, asymmetrical spacing, and bold Serif typography (Bodoni Moda) to establish an authoritative and high-end presence.
- **Memorable Motif:** The repetition of copper rules and sharp, non-rounded edges reflects the precision and attention to detail Nate brings to every vehicle.
- **User Experience:** The flow prioritizes credibility early on, followed by curated services, before ending with a streamlined quote request form to convert high-intent visitors.

The site is fully responsive, semantically structured, and ready to serve as the definitive digital home for Gloss n Glow in Omaha.

## Brand & Style

This design system embodies "High-Octane Luxury" through a fusion of **Editorial Minimalism** and **Industrial Precision**. It is designed for a discerning clientele who views automotive maintenance as an art form rather than a chore. The visual language is stark, authoritative, and meticulously structured.

The aesthetic leans heavily into a **Modern Brutalist-Luxury** hybrid. It rejects the softness of common consumer apps in favor of sharp 0px corners, high-contrast typography, and a rigid architectural grid. Motion should be swift and linear, mimicking the acceleration of a high-performance vehicle. Subtle grain overlays on dark surfaces provide a tactile, premium feel reminiscent of high-end automotive interiors and matte finishes.

## Colors

The palette is rooted in the "Midnight Onyx" (#0A0A0A) workspace to reflect the exclusivity of a private garage or luxury showroom. 

- **Primary (Brushed Copper):** Used exclusively for high-priority actions, accents, and structural hair lines. It represents the "craftsmanship" aspect of the brand.
- **Secondary (Midnight Onyx):** The foundational canvas. It provides deep contrast and a premium backdrop for photography.
- **Surface (Slate Grey):** Used for subtle layering and container separation to prevent a purely flat appearance.
- **Functional (Crisp White):** Reserved for primary text and iconography to ensure maximum legibility against the dark theme.

## Typography

The typography system creates an editorial rhythm through extreme contrast. 

**Bodoni Moda** serves as the display typeface. Its high vertical contrast and sharp serifs evoke the feeling of a premium fashion or automotive journal. It should be used for headings and impactful pull-quotes.

**Montserrat** provides a functional, wide-tracked counterbalance. By increasing letter spacing (tracking) on body and label text, we achieve an "architectural" feel that remains legible at all sizes. All labels and navigation items must be set in All-Caps to maintain the formal, structured tone of the design system.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system utilizes a **Fixed-Grid Modular Layout**. The interface is partitioned by thin 1px "hairlines" in Brushed Copper or Slate Grey, creating a containerized look that mimics a technical blueprint or a high-end editorial spread.

- **Grid:** A 12-column grid on desktop, transitioning to a 4-column grid on mobile.
- **Rhythm:** Spacing is strictly based on 8px increments (2 units), ensuring mathematical precision.
- **Hairlines:** Instead of standard margins or shadows, use vertical and horizontal rules to separate content sections. These lines should extend to the edge of the viewport where possible to emphasize the structural grid.
- **White Space:** Generous vertical padding (80px - 120px) between major sections is mandatory to allow the large-scale photography to "breathe."

## Elevation & Depth

In keeping with the "Meticulous Craftsmanship" theme, the design system avoids traditional shadows. Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Architectural Rules**.

1.  **Base Layer:** Midnight Onyx (#0A0A0A).
2.  **Elevated Surfaces:** Slate Grey (#2D2D2D) used for cards or secondary navigation bars, appearing to be "inset" or "machined" into the base layer.
3.  **The Rule System:** Depth is signaled by the presence of the 1px Copper hairline. An element "above" another is separated by a sharp line rather than a blur.
4.  **Photography:** Use high-contrast, low-key lighting in imagery to create natural depth within the layout. Images should feel integrated into the grid, often acting as background surfaces themselves.

## Shapes

The shape language is defined by **Absolute Precision**. There are no rounded corners in this design system. Every button, input field, card, and image container must utilize a 0px radius (Sharp).

This hard-edged approach reflects the "high-octane" nature of the brand—precision engineering and clinical attention to detail. Buttons should be rectangular, and hover states should involve sharp color fills or the appearance of a 1px border.

## Components

### Buttons
Primary buttons are solid Brushed Copper with white or black All-Caps Montserrat text. Secondary buttons are "Ghost" style: a 1px white or copper border with no fill. All buttons have 0px corner radius and generous horizontal padding.

### Chips & Tags
Used for service categories (e.g., "CERAMIC COATING"). Small, All-Caps text with a 1px Slate Grey border. No fill.

### Input Fields
Underline-only or fully boxed with a 1px Slate Grey border. Labels stay above the field in `label-caps` style. Focus state changes the border/underline to Brushed Copper.

### Cards
Cards are defined by their borders rather than background color. A 1px Slate Grey outline with 0px corners. On hover, the border may transition to Brushed Copper.

### Lists
Service lists should be separated by horizontal 1px rules (hairlines). Use the Bodoni serif for service titles and Montserrat for descriptions to maintain the editorial hierarchy.

### Editorial Dividers
Used to separate major sections. A horizontal line in Brushed Copper, often accompanied by a small numeric indicator (e.g., "01", "02") in the `label-caps` style.
