## Brand & Style

This design system establishes a high-end, editorial visual identity for boutique pet care. It moves away from the typical "cartoonish" aesthetic of the pet industry, opting instead for a sophisticated, magazine-inspired look that balances professional reliability with the energetic joy of canine companionship.

The style is characterized by **Editorial Minimalism** blended with **Organic Geometry**. It leverages heavy whitespace and asymmetric layouts to create a sense of premium curation. The brand personality is authoritative yet warm—reassuring owners that their pets are in expert hands while celebrating the active, outdoor nature of the service.

Key visual pillars include:
- **Asymmetry:** Intentional placement of elements off-center to drive visual interest.
- **Organic Masks:** Using "pebble" and "blob" shapes for imagery to soften the high-contrast color palette.
- **Typographic Scale:** Dramatic contrast between large display serifs and clean, modern sans-serif functional text.

## Colors

The palette, inspired by natural warmth and sophisticated depth, is anchored by **Biscuit** as the primary surface color to provide a softer, more premium feel than pure white.

- **San Antonio Sun (#D2691E):** Used as the primary action color. It represents energy, leather leashes, and the Golden Hour in the park. Use it for primary buttons, active states, and key accents.
- **Deep Paw (#1B1B1E):** The grounding force. This navy-charcoal provides the "authority" and is used for primary typography and high-contrast UI elements to ensure accessibility and readability.
- **Biscuit (#FDF5E6):** The core canvas. This cream tone should be used for background surfaces and containers to create a warm, inviting environment.
- **Neutral (#4A4A4A):** A secondary grey used exclusively for long-form body text and secondary labels to reduce visual vibration against the cream background.

## Typography

The typography strategy relies on the tension between the classic elegance of **Playfair Display** and the wide, modern openness of **Montserrat**.

**Headlines** should use Playfair Display. For large display moments, use tight letter-spacing and high-weight variants to emphasize the high-contrast strokes of the typeface.

**Body and Functional Text** utilize Montserrat. The wide apertures of this font enhance readability and provide a "breathable" feel that complements the generous whitespace of the layout. 

**Labels and Navigation** items should frequently use uppercase Montserrat with increased letter-spacing (tracking) to establish a clear hierarchy and a "high-fashion" editorial aesthetic.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system employs a **12-column fluid grid** with significant horizontal margins to enforce an editorial "frame" around the content.

- **Asymmetric Balance:** Instead of centering all content, align text blocks to the left 6 columns while allowing organic image "pebbles" to float or bleed off the right edge.
- **Vertical Rhythm:** A strict 8px baseline is used. Section gaps are intentionally large (120px+) to allow the brand's "Biscuit" background to act as a negative space element, elevating the perceived value of the services.
- **Mobile Reflow:** On mobile, margins compress to 20px, and the 12-column grid collapses to a single-column stack. Maintain the large vertical gaps between sections even on smaller screens to preserve the premium feel.

## Elevation & Depth

To maintain a "boutique" aesthetic, this design system avoids heavy drop shadows and standard material depth. Instead, it uses **Tonal Layering** and **Soft Ambient Occlusion**.

- **Surface Levels:** Use the 'Biscuit' color as the base. Higher elevation elements (like cards or floating navigation) use a slightly lighter version of the base or a very subtle 1px border in 'Deep Paw' at 5% opacity.
- **Shadows:** When necessary for functional depth (e.g., primary buttons or modals), use "Long & Soft" shadows. These are defined by a large blur radius (30px+), very low opacity (10% or less), and a slight color tint using the 'Deep Paw' navy rather than pure black.
- **Organic Depth:** Depth is also conveyed through the overlapping of organic "pebble" shapes. Use z-index layering where a photo mask might sit slightly over a text block to create a tactile, collage-like feel.

## Shapes

The shape language is the core differentiator of the design system. It moves away from standard rectangles in favor of **Organic Pebbles**.

- **Image Masks:** Photos of dogs and trainers should never be simple rectangles. Use custom SVG "blob" paths that mimic smooth river stones or soft paw prints.
- **UI Elements:** Standard components like cards and input fields follow the `rounded-lg` (1rem) and `rounded-xl` (1.5rem) settings to maintain a soft, approachable feel.
- **Buttons:** Use high-radius or pill-shaped buttons to contrast against the sharp, high-contrast serif headlines.

## Components

### Buttons
Primary buttons are pill-shaped, using 'San Antonio Sun' with white or 'Biscuit' text. They should feel "squishy" and inviting. Secondary buttons use a 'Deep Paw' outline with Montserrat Medium uppercase text.

### Cards
Cards should be used sparingly. When used, they feature a 1px 'Deep Paw' (at 10% opacity) border with no shadow, or a solid 'Biscuit' fill that is slightly darker than the page background to create a subtle recessed look.

### Pebble Masks
A signature component. This is a container for images that uses a CSS `clip-path` or SVG mask. These masks should be slightly irregular—never a perfect circle or oval—to mimic natural, organic paths.

### Input Fields
Inputs use a minimalist approach: a single bottom border in 'Deep Paw' or a fully rounded 'Biscuit' container with a soft internal shadow to suggest a "carved" look.

### Chips & Tags
Used for "Training Types" or "Dog Breeds." These should be small, pill-shaped, and use the 'Deep Paw' background with 'Biscuit' text to pop against the light page background.

### Editorial Accents
Include "Pull Quotes" using Playfair Display Italic and decorative "asymmetric lines" (thin 1px horizontal rules that only span 3 columns) to separate content blocks.
