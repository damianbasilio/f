I've designed a bespoke, industrial-editorial homepage for **419 Towing & Auto Transport LLC**. 

### Design Rationale:
- **Visual Identity:** I moved away from generic towing templates to create a "Steel & Hazard" theme. The interface uses a deep charcoal base with high-visibility orange accents, reflecting the grit and urgency of the towing industry.
- **Editorial Layout:** The "Diagonal Pull" motif is integrated through angled buttons and geometric section framing, creating a sense of mechanical movement.
- **Typography:** I used heavy, condensed sans-serif headings to project strength and authority, paired with clean, spacious body copy for professional readability.
- **Trust-First Architecture:** The "Credibility" and "About" sections are placed high in the hierarchy to establish the business as a permanent, local Toledo fixture rather than just an anonymous service.

The design includes all required technical specs, such as the map slot at 626 Scott Street and a functional contact manifest.

How would you like to proceed? We could add some motion effects to the industrial elements or refine the fleet showcase.

## Brand & Style
The brand personality is authoritative, rugged, and premium—positioning the service not just as a local utility, but as a high-tier logistics operation. It evokes a sense of mechanical power and unwavering reliability, tailored for a Toledo-based audience that values hard work and precision.

The design style is a fusion of **Industrial Brutalism** and **High-Contrast Editorial**. It utilizes heavy geometric forms, intentional structural tension, and a "The Diagonal Pull" motif. This visual narrative uses 45-degree angles to suggest movement, tension, and the physics of towing. Surfaces should feel heavy and tactile, utilizing subtle asphalt textures and steel-grain overlays to differentiate from generic digital interfaces.

## Colors
The palette is built on "Steel & Hazard." **Hazard Orange (#FF6B00)** is the high-visibility driver for action, used exclusively for primary CTAs, critical status indicators, and the "Diagonal Pull" accents. 

**Deep Charcoal (#1A1A1A)** serves as the primary canvas, providing a heavy, "night-shift" industrial backdrop that feels more premium than pure black. **Concrete Grey (#E5E5E5)** is used for secondary surfaces and high-readability typography, cutting through the darkness with the clarity of a shop floor. A mid-tone **Neutral (#2A2A2A)** is employed for container strokes and subtle depth layering.

## Typography
The typography system is designed for maximum impact and readability under pressure. **Archivo Narrow** is the core industrial voice; it is used in heavy weights and all-caps for headlines to mimic the stenciled lettering found on heavy machinery and transport crates.

**Inter** provides a clean, systematic contrast for body text, ensuring that technical details, pricing, and service descriptions are legible and trustworthy. Display sizes utilize tight letter-spacing and aggressive line-heights to create a dense, editorial aesthetic that feels powerful and intentional.

## Layout & Spacing
This design system uses a **12-column fixed-width grid** for desktop and a **4-column fluid grid** for mobile. The layout philosophy is defined by "The Diagonal Pull," where sections are often separated by 45-degree diagonal dividers or offset containers that break the traditional horizontal rhythm.

Hero sections and brand-story modules use dramatic, expansive whitespace (the "Section Gap") to convey premium quality. Conversely, utility-driven sections like fleet specs or service lists use a high-density grid with tight 8px units to imply efficiency and professional rigor.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is created through **bold borders** and **tonal layering** rather than soft shadows. Surfaces are stacked using distinct shades of Charcoal and Neutral to create a "machined" look. 

To reinforce the industrial theme:
- **Primary Elevation:** Use 2px solid strokes in Concrete Grey or Hazard Orange for containers.
- **The Offset Shadow:** Instead of blur, use a hard, 4px solid offset shadow in Primary Orange to make buttons and active cards "pop."
- **Overlays:** A subtle noise or "grain" texture is applied to the base background to simulate asphalt or steel surfaces, preventing the UI from feeling "flat" or "plastic."

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px)**. Rounded corners are avoided to maintain the "Heavy Metal" aesthetic. Precision is communicated through crisp 90-degree angles and 45-degree clipped corners on buttons and badges. 

Incorporate "The Diagonal Pull" motif by using CSS clip-paths or SVGs to create skewed container edges, suggesting the angle of a tow bed or a tensioned cable.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Solid Hazard Orange background with Charcoal text. Use a 45-degree clipped top-right corner for the "Primary" action button. On hover, the button shifts slightly (4px) with a hard stroke shadow.
- **Cards:** Heavy 2px Concrete Grey borders with a subtle grain texture overlay. Titles should be in the Archive Narrow "Label-Bold" style.
- **Input Fields:** Dark Charcoal backgrounds with a bottom-only 2px Concrete Grey stroke that turns Hazard Orange on focus. 
- **Chips/Badges:** Small, all-caps labels with a 1px border. Used for "Available 24/7" or "Toledo Local" tags.
- **The Tension Line:** A custom horizontal rule component that consists of a thin grey line interrupted by a 45-degree Hazard Orange slash, used to separate content blocks.
- **Fleet Lists:** Use a high-density table format with alternating Charcoal and slightly lighter Neutral row colors, reminiscent of a manifest or technical logbook.
