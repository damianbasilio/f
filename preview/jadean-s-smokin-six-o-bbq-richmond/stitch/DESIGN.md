## Brand & Style
The design system reflects a "Premium Rustic" aesthetic, moving away from typical backyard BBQ tropes toward a sophisticated, chef-driven editorial experience. It targets a discerning audience that values the craft of slow-smoked meats and the heritage of Richmond's culinary scene. 

The style combines **Minimalism** with **Tactile** influences. It utilizes vast amounts of "Butcher Paper Cream" whitespace to allow high-fidelity food photography to serve as the primary texture. The interface is structured with thin, architectural lines—reminiscent of cast-iron grates and kitchen blueprints—creating a sense of "intentional density" without clutter. The emotional response is one of warmth, authority, and meticulous craft.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in the physical environment of a smokehouse. 
- **Charcoal Embers (#1A1A1A):** Used for primary typography, structural borders, and deep-fill backgrounds to provide weight and authority.
- **Slow Burn Orange (#D95D39):** Used sparingly as a "heat" accent for call-to-actions, price points, and critical notifications.
- **Patina Copper (#A67C52):** Utilized for secondary accents, iconography, and subtle flourishes to suggest aged metal and tradition.
- **Butcher Paper Cream (#F9F6F0):** The foundational surface color, providing a soft, organic warmth that is less clinical than pure white.

## Typography
The typography strategy relies on the tension between the high-contrast elegance of **Playfair Display** and the modern, geometric stability of **Montserrat**. 

Headlines should utilize "display" settings with tight letter-spacing to emphasize the editorial feel. The `label-caps` style is essential for navigational elements and small descriptors, acting as a modern counterpoint to the traditional serif headings. All body text should maintain generous line-heights to ensure the "premium" feeling of a high-end menu or magazine.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **fluid grid** model with an emphasis on asymmetry. Hero sections and key narrative blocks should intentionally break the grid—for example, overlapping an image of smoked brisket with a text block that is offset by 2-3 columns.

- **Desktop:** A 12-column grid with wide 64px margins to create an "airy" boutique feel.
- **Mobile:** A 4-column grid with tight margins, focusing on vertical stacks and high-impact imagery.
- **Structural Lines:** Use 1px "Charcoal Embers" borders (opacity 15-20%) to separate sections, mimicking the precision of a chef's workspace.

## Elevation & Depth
This design system avoids traditional drop shadows in favor of **Tonal Layers** and **Overlays**.

- **Depth through Overlap:** Create hierarchy by layering typography over images with subtle "Charcoal" gradients to ensure legibility.
- **Surface Tiers:** Use "Butcher Paper Cream" as the base, with occasional "Charcoal" containers for high-contrast callouts.
- **The '6.0' Motif:** Implement the signature graphic as a low-opacity watermark background or as a "stamped" element on cards, creating a subtle third dimension of brand identity.

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0)**. To maintain the artisanal, "butcher-block" aesthetic, avoid rounded corners on buttons, images, or containers. The precision of right angles conveys the sharpness of a knife and the structural integrity of the pit. 

Small exceptions are made for iconography or the "6.0" graphic motif, which should remain organic and circular to contrast against the rigid UI layout.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Rectangular with 1px borders. Primary buttons use a solid "Charcoal" fill with "Cream" text. Secondary buttons use "Patina Copper" text with a 1px copper border. Hover states should involve a subtle shift to "Slow Burn Orange."
- **Cards:** Borderless with high-impact photography. Text should be placed directly on the "Cream" background below the image or as an asymmetrical overlay.
- **Inputs:** Simple bottom-border only (1px Charcoal), creating a minimalist, "signed" look.
- **The Menu List:** Traditional list style but with "Patina Copper" price points and "Playfair Display" item titles. Use thin horizontal rules between items.
- **Signature '6.0' Badge:** A circular floating element or fixed corner graphic used to denote "Chef's Specials" or "Limited Batches."
