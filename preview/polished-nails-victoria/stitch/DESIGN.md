I have designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Polished Nails**. This design moves away from standard local-business templates to create a premium, high-fashion aesthetic that emphasizes the brand's meticulous artistry.

### Design Highlights:
*   **Editorial Layout:** I used an asymmetrical grid with generous whitespace and thin 1px architectural rules to evoke the feel of a luxury fashion magazine.
*   **Sophisticated Palette:** The visual identity is built on a "Midnight & Champagne" theme (deep charcoal and warm beige) with a striking "Lacquer Red" accent for calls to action.
*   **Typographic Hierarchy:** High-contrast serif headlines (Bodoni Moda) provide an air of exclusivity, while clean sans-serif labels maintain professional clarity.
*   **Custom Sections:**
    *   **The Gallery Catalog:** Services are presented as a curated collection rather than a standard price list.
    *   **Visit Studio:** A dedicated space for studio details featuring the requested interactive map slot.
    *   **Minimalist Inquiries:** A refined contact form designed to feel like a concierge request.

The site is fully responsive, featuring a mobile-ready navigation menu and production-ready HTML/CSS.

## Brand & Style

The brand personality is rooted in the intersection of high-fashion editorial and boutique hospitality. It targets a discerning clientele in Victoria, TX, who value precision, luxury, and a curated experience. The design system evokes an emotional response of exclusivity and meticulous care.

The chosen style is **Modern Minimalist Editorial**. This aesthetic prioritizes "intentional tension" through generous, asymmetric whitespace and a rigid adherence to a thin-line architectural motif. Every element is treated as a piece of a high-end magazine layout, moving away from standard app patterns toward a more sophisticated, gallery-like presentation.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by the contrast between **Midnight** (#1A1A1A) and **Champagne** (#F4EFE6). 

- **Champagne** serves as the primary canvas, providing a warm, skin-toned backdrop that feels more inviting than stark white.
- **Midnight** is used for primary typography and architectural lines, ensuring high legibility and a grounded, authoritative feel.
- **Lacquer Red** (#A82B2B) is the critical accent, reserved strictly for primary actions, high-importance highlights, and "the pop of color" reminiscent of a fresh manicure.
- **Neutral** tones are used for secondary text and borders to maintain a soft hierarchy against the Champagne background.

## Typography

The typography system relies on a high-contrast pairing to signify luxury. 

**Bodoni Moda** is the voice of the brand. It is used for all headlines and display text. Its sharp serifs and extreme stroke contrast mimic the mastheads of fashion bibles. 

**Montserrat** provides a functional, modern counterpoint. For this design system, Montserrat should be used with slightly increased letter-spacing (`0.01em` to `0.15em`) to evoke a sense of breathing room and airiness. Use `label-caps` for small descriptors, section headers, and eyebrow text to maintain a structured, architectural feel.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system utilizes a **Fixed Grid** with intentional asymmetry. On desktop, the layout is based on a 12-column grid, but content is often pushed to the right or left with large "tension gaps" to create an editorial feel.

- **Vertical Rhythm:** Built on an 8px base unit.
- **The "Tension" Rule:** Avoid centering content within the container. Align primary imagery to the far right or left, and place typography in the opposing quadrant with significant whitespace between them.
- **Mobile Adaptation:** On mobile, the layout collapses to a single column, but maintains large vertical margins (64px+) between sections to ensure the "exclusive" feeling is not lost to clutter.
- **Architectural Lines:** Use 1px Midnight or soft grey lines to separate sections horizontally, often extending only 50-70% of the container width rather than spanning the full width.

## Elevation & Depth

To maintain a "flat editorial" aesthetic, this design system avoids traditional drop shadows and neomorphism. Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Line Work**.

- **Surfaces:** Use the Champagne background as the base. Deep Midnight surfaces are used to "anchor" specific sections (like the footer or a callout gallery).
- **Outlines:** Instead of shadows, use 1px "ghost borders" in a slightly darker shade of Champagne or a very light Midnight (low opacity) to define cards or input fields.
- **Overlays:** Imagery may occasionally overlap text blocks or other images to create a sense of physical layering found in magazine collages.

## Shapes

The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px)**. 

Curves are avoided to maintain the architectural, sophisticated tone of the brand. Rectangular frames, sharp-cornered buttons, and square image containers reinforce the feeling of precision and meticulousness inherent in a high-end nail salon. The only "round" elements should be photography of the nails themselves or circular decorative motifs (like a wax seal), providing a soft contrast to the rigid UI.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are sharp-edged rectangles with a Lacquer Red background and white text. Secondary buttons use a Midnight outline with no fill. Both utilize the `button` typography style (all-caps, wide-tracked).
- **Cards:** Cards for services or gallery items should have 0px corner radius. Use 1px thin borders. Ensure imagery takes up at least 70% of the card area.
- **Input Fields:** Minimalist design with only a bottom border (1px Midnight). Labels should use the `label-caps` style positioned above the line.
- **Lists:** Service menus should be styled like a table of contents. Left-align the service name in Bodoni Moda, and right-align the price. Connect them with a very thin, faint dotted line if the horizontal gap is large.
- **Chips/Tags:** Used for nail shape or color categories. Sharp rectangles with a Champagne-tinted background and Midnight text.
- **Architectural Dividers:** Use vertical 1px lines to separate sidebar content from main content, echoing the "thin architectural line" motif.
