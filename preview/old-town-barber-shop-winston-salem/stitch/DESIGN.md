I've designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Old Town Barber Shop**. 

This design avoids the generic layouts of typical local business sites in favor of a "Modern Heritage" aesthetic—blending the gritty tradition of a classic barbershop with the sophisticated whitespace and typographic hierarchy of a premium magazine.

### Design Rationale:
*   **Typography as Identity**: I used oversized, high-contrast serif headlines to establish an immediate sense of authority and timelessness. 
*   **The "Rule" Motif**: Thin, elegant lines are used throughout the design to separate sections and guide the eye, creating a structured yet open feel.
*   **Curated Visuals**: I integrated moody, desaturated imagery that focuses on craftsmanship—the precision of a blade, the texture of a vintage chair—to sell an experience rather than just a haircut.
*   **Layout Fluidity**: Instead of standard card grids, I opted for an asymmetrical editorial flow, ensuring the site feels unique and high-end.

The site is production-ready, featuring a responsive navigation menu, smooth scroll interactions, and a custom map slot ready for integration.
