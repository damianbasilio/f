I have completed the bespoke homepage for **Overall Mobile Mechanic**, moving away from standard templates to create a high-end **Industrial Editorial** experience. 

### Design Rationale:
*   **Visual Identity:** Built on a "Carbon and Safety Orange" palette, reflecting both the grit of automotive repair and the precision of modern diagnostics. 
*   **Typography:** Used bold, condensed headers with overlapping background text ("OVERALL") to create a memorable, editorial-grade hero section.
*   **Asymmetrical Layout:** Breaking the traditional grid, I used varying card sizes and thin "precision rules" (lines) to guide the eye through the services, suggesting a custom, high-touch service rather than a generic shop.
*   **Local Authority:** Sections like "The Pensacola Standard" and the prominent "24/7 Diagnostics" ticker emphasize local expertise and immediate reliability.
*   **Functional Polish:** Included the required map slot and a high-contrast, minimalist contact form designed for high conversion.

The site is fully responsive and features smooth-scroll navigation and interactive hover states that reinforce the mechanical theme.

## Brand & Style
The design system for this mobile automotive service project establishes a high-end, "Industrial Editorial" identity. It departs from traditional greasy-shop aesthetics in favor of a sophisticated, technical, and precise visual language. The brand targets a discerning audience in Pensacola who values mechanical expertise paired with professional transparency.

The style combines **Minimalism** and **Brutalism** with a refined, magazine-like layout. It utilizes intentional asymmetry, massive overlapping typography, and "precision rules"—ultra-thin 1px lines that act as structural guides. The emotional response should be one of absolute confidence: the feeling of a high-performance workshop brought directly to the client's driveway.

## Colors
The palette is rooted in a "Deep Carbon" ecosystem, utilizing a dark-mode-first approach to evoke the professional atmosphere of high-end automotive engineering.

- **Deep Carbon (#1A1A1A):** The primary foundation. Used for backgrounds and primary surfaces to create a sense of depth and premium quality.
- **Safety Orange (#FF5F1F):** The high-velocity accent. Reserved for critical calls to action, active states, and "precision rules" that highlight mechanical accuracy.
- **Concrete Grey (#E5E5E5):** The primary typographic and foreground color. It provides a stark, legible contrast against the carbon base.
- **Surface Tiers:** Use #262626 for secondary containers to maintain a subtle, tonal distinction without relying on shadows.

## Typography
The typography system is the core of the "Editorial" feel. It uses **Archivo Narrow** for headlines—a bold, condensed sans-serif that feels impactful and urgent, reminiscent of industrial signage but refined for the screen. **Hanken Grotesk** is used for body copy to ensure high readability and a contemporary, tech-forward tone.

Large-scale headlines should frequently overlap container boundaries or background imagery to create a sense of movement. Use "label-caps" for all technical metadata and small UI labels to reinforce the "spec sheet" aesthetic.

## Layout & Spacing
This design system utilizes a **Fixed Grid** for desktop (12 columns) and a **Fluid Grid** for mobile (4 columns). 

- **Staggered Masonry:** Instead of uniform cards, services and gallery items should be placed in a staggered layout. Odd-numbered items should have a slight vertical offset (e.g., 40px) to break the horizontal line.
- **Precision Rules:** Use 1px "Safety Orange" or "Concrete Grey" lines (low opacity) to delineate sections and connect related data points, mimicking technical blueprints.
- **Asymmetry:** Content blocks should often be offset from the center. For example, a headline might span columns 1-8 while the supporting body text is tucked into columns 5-10.

## Elevation & Depth
In this design system, depth is achieved through **Tonal Layers** and **Geometric Overlaps** rather than traditional shadows.

1.  **Planimetric Depth:** Elements are layered like sheets of metal or blueprints. A text block may sit directly on top of a 1px rule, which sits on top of a dark surface.
2.  **No Shadows:** Avoid soft blurs. If depth is required, use a 1px solid border in a slightly lighter shade of grey or a "hard shadow" offset by 4px with 100% opacity in Safety Orange to create a graphic pop.
3.  **Background Blurs:** When overlays are necessary (e.g., mobile menus), use a heavy backdrop blur on a #1A1A1A surface with 80% opacity to maintain the industrial-glass feel.

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0)**. There are no rounded corners in this design system. 

Every button, input field, and image container must have 0px border-radius. This reinforces the "Industrial" narrative, suggesting precision-cut components and structural rigidity. Use 45-degree chamfered corners for special callouts or "active" service indicators to mimic mechanical detailing.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid "Safety Orange" with "Deep Carbon" text in bold Hanken Grotesk. They have no radius. Hover states should invert the colors or add a 1px "Concrete Grey" offset outline.
- **Service Tiles:** Staggered boxes with a 1px "Concrete Grey" border at 20% opacity. Titles should be in Archivo Narrow, positioned in the top-left, with a "precision rule" extending from the title to the edge of the container.
- **Input Fields:** Bottom-border only, 1px "Concrete Grey". Label-caps sit 8px above the line. Focus state turns the border "Safety Orange".
- **Chips/Status Indicators:** Sharp-edged boxes with 1px borders. Use "Safety Orange" for active/critical status and "Concrete Grey" for neutral/completed status.
- **The "Accuracy Marker":** A custom UI component consisting of a small "+" crosshair (Safety Orange) used to highlight specific focal points on images or as a bullet point replacement in lists.
