I've completed the first professional homepage for **Playdates Petsitting**, built entirely from their Facebook presence and localized for the Myrtle Beach community.

### Design Highlights:
*   **Brand Identity:** I established the "Sun-Drenched Sanctuary" design system, using a warm amber and deep forest green palette to evoke trust, nature, and the sunny atmosphere of Myrtle Beach.
*   **Copy & Voice:** The headlines and sections directly echo the Facebook page's warm tone, including the "Your Pets Deserve All the Kisses 🥰" tagline.
*   **Service Area Focus:** The landing page explicitly highlights coverage from 38th Ave N. to Briarcliffe Acres, ensuring local pet owners know they are in the right place.
*   **Production-Ready Structure:**
    *   **Hero Section:** A clear value proposition and immediate CTA for the "Meet & Greet."
    *   **Services:** Dedicated cards for drop-ins and overnight stays.
    *   **Credibility:** Social proof highlights including the 5-star rating and "Always Open" availability.
    *   **Map Slot:** A semantic, accessible map container ready for integration.
    *   **Contact & Footer:** Fully functional contact form UI and localized footer with Demi's direct contact details.

The site is fully responsive and uses the modern **Outfit** typeface to maintain a clean, professional look. How does this look for Playdates' first dedicated web presence?

## Brand & Style
The brand personality is rooted in the "neighborly professional"—someone you trust with your home and your most beloved companions. It balances the warmth of the Myrtle Beach sun with the reliability of a premium service provider.

The design style is **Modern Organic**. It avoids the clinical coldness of tech-first platforms in favor of a soft, tactile interface that feels human and approachable. By utilizing generous whitespace and a "soft-UI" approach, the design system evokes feelings of safety, joy, and local community. It is professional enough for high-end clients yet warm enough for first-time pet owners.

## Colors
The palette is inspired by the intersection of the beach and the park. 

- **Primary (Golden Amber):** Used for primary actions and highlights. It represents energy, warmth, and the golden coat of a happy dog.
- **Secondary (Park Green):** Used for typography and structural elements where trust and stability are required. It provides a grounding contrast to the amber.
- **Tertiary (Soft Moss):** A low-saturation green used for subtle backgrounds, success states, and secondary container fills.
- **Background (Cream):** A warm off-white that reduces eye strain and feels more inviting than pure white.

## Typography
The typographic hierarchy uses **Outfit** for all display and interface elements to maintain a friendly, modern geometric look. Its rounded characteristics mirror the "friendly" nature of the brand.

For long-form content, service descriptions, and trust-building narratives, **Source Serif 4** is used. This serif choice provides a literary weight and academic reliability, ensuring that the business is perceived as knowledgeable and established. Body text should always be set in the Secondary Park Green or a high-contrast neutral to ensure maximum legibility for all age groups.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fluid Content Grid** with a maximum container width of 1280px for desktop.

- **Desktop:** 12-column grid with 24px gutters. Content is centered with 64px outside margins.
- **Tablet:** 8-column grid with 20px gutters.
- **Mobile:** 4-column grid with 16px gutters and 16px margins.

The spacing rhythm is based on an 8px root. Use "generous" vertical rhythm (LG and XL units) between sections to allow the brand photography and icons to breathe, creating a sense of calm and organization.

## Elevation & Depth
This design system utilizes **Ambient Shadows** to create a soft, sun-drenched feel. 

- **Surface Level 0:** The main Cream background (#FAF9F6).
- **Surface Level 1:** White cards with a very soft, diffused shadow (Blur: 20px, Y: 4px, Color: #2D5A27 at 5% opacity). The use of the Secondary Green in the shadow tint keeps the depth looking natural rather than grey/synthetic.
- **Interaction:** On hover, cards should slightly lift (increase shadow spread and decrease Y offset) to provide tactile feedback.

Avoid heavy inner shadows or sharp black drop shadows. Depth should feel like light passing through a window.

## Shapes
The shape language is consistently **Rounded**. There are no sharp corners in this design system. 

- **Standard Elements:** Buttons and small input fields use a 0.5rem (8px) radius.
- **Featured Elements:** Main cards and content containers use a 1rem (16px) radius.
- **Playful Elements:** Small tags or "Pet Profiles" may use the `rounded-xl` (24px) or full pill-shape to emphasize the friendly nature of the business.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use the Golden Amber background with White or Park Green text. They should have a subtle 1px inner stroke of a darker amber to give them a "pillowy" feel.
- **Input Fields:** Use the Tertiary Moss color for borders in a neutral state. When focused, the border transitions to Park Green with a soft outer glow.
- **Cards:** White backgrounds with Level 1 elevation. Cards for "Available Sitters" or "Pet Gallery" should include a generous 16px padding.
- **Chips/Tags:** Used for pet attributes (e.g., "High Energy", "Senior Care"). Use pill shapes with a light Tertiary Moss fill and Park Green text.
- **Pet Status Indicator:** A custom component—a small circular badge with a paw-print icon—used to show real-time updates (e.g., "Just Walked", "Fed").
- **Lists:** Use custom icons (miniature paws or suns) instead of standard bullets to maintain the local, loving brand tone.
