## Brand & Style
The design system is engineered to position automotive detailing as a high-tier luxury service rather than a utility. The brand personality is clinical, sophisticated, and elite, targeting high-net-worth individuals who view their vehicles as investments. 

The aesthetic direction merges **Technical Minimalism** with **Editorial Luxury**. It utilizes heavy whitespace (or "dark space"), ultra-thin strokes, and a rigid adherence to a grid to evoke the feeling of a high-end watchmaker or an architectural firm. The emotional response should be one of absolute trust in technical mastery and surgical precision.

## Colors
This design system utilizes a deep, immersive dark mode to reflect the "Midnight Obsidian" finish of a premium paint job.

- **Midnight Obsidian (#0A0C10):** The primary foundation. Used for all deep surfaces and backgrounds to provide maximum contrast for the accent.
- **Electric Teal (#00F2FF):** The technical pulse. Used sparingly for critical calls to action, active states, and precision data points.
- **Cool Slate (#4A5568):** The structural bridge. Used for secondary text, subtle borders, and inactive UI elements.
- **Bright White (#F8FAFC):** The clarity layer. Used for primary typography and high-signal icons to ensure maximum readability against the dark void.

## Typography
The typography strategy contrasts technical geometry with utilitarian clarity. 

**Space Grotesk** serves as the display face. Its idiosyncratic, mechanical letterforms suggest engineering and "precision lines." Use it for headlines and data-heavy labels. Larger sizes should use tighter tracking to maintain a "machined" look.

**Inter** provides the functional balance. It is used for all long-form body copy and descriptions, ensuring the technical aesthetic never compromises the user's ability to digest service tiers and pricing.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **strict 12-column fixed grid** on desktop and a **4-column fluid grid** on mobile. 

The design emphasizes "The Void"—wide horizontal gutters and significant vertical gaps between sections to allow the imagery of detailed cars to breathe. Elements should often be aligned to the far left or right edges of the grid to create a sophisticated, asymmetrical editorial flow. 

Precision rule lines (0.5px thickness in Cool Slate) should be used to separate content blocks, mimicking technical blueprints or laser-leveling lines.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is expressed through **Tonal Layers** rather than traditional shadows. Shadows are largely avoided to maintain the sharp, flat, technical aesthetic.

- **Level 0 (Surface):** Midnight Obsidian (#0A0C10).
- **Level 1 (Cards/Inputs):** A slightly raised slate tint (#161B22) with a 1px solid border in Cool Slate at 30% opacity.
- **Level 2 (Modals):** Same as Level 1 but with a 1px Electric Teal top-border to indicate focus.

Interaction is signaled through "Light Glows"—a subtle 15px blur of Electric Teal positioned behind primary buttons to simulate high-intensity LED garage lighting.

## Shapes
The shape language is **unapologetically sharp (0px)**. 

To reinforce the concept of "Precision Lines," all containers, buttons, and input fields must feature 90-degree corners. This evokes surgical tools, carbon fiber panels, and professional detailing bays. Softness is avoided entirely to distance the brand from the "bubbly" aesthetic of consumer-grade car washes.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are "Electric Teal" with "Midnight Obsidian" text, using the `label-caps` style. They feature no rounding. On hover, they transition to a "ghost" state: transparent background with an Electric Teal 1px border.
- **Precision Lines (Rules):** Vertical and horizontal 0.5px lines in Cool Slate are used to frame content. Every section title should be preceded by a horizontal rule that extends 40px beyond the text.
- **Cards:** Used for service packages. They feature a 1px border on all sides. When a package is "Selected," the border glows with an Electric Teal inner stroke.
- **Service Chips:** Small, rectangular labels used to denote car sizes (SUV, Sedan, Exotic). These use the `label-caps` typography and are outlined in Cool Slate.
- **Inputs:** Underlined-only fields (bottom border only) to maintain a minimalist, architectural feel. The label sits above the line in `label-caps`.
- **Progress Indicators:** Linear, thin bars that use Electric Teal to show the status of a detailing appointment.
