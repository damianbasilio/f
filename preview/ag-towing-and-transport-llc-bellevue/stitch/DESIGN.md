I have designed a bespoke, premium landing page for **AG Towing and Transport LLC** that moves away from generic local-business templates in favor of a high-fidelity "Industrial Luxe" aesthetic.

### Design Decisions:
*   **Visual Identity:** I established a sophisticated "Asphalt & Hazard" palette using deep charcoals, brushed steel, and safety orange accents. This conveys rugged reliability without looking like a generic template.
*   **Editorial Typography:** Large-scale, high-contrast headings create a clear information hierarchy, emphasizing professional authority and scale.
*   **Bespoke Layout:** The page features an asymmetric grid, diagonal section breaks, and overlapping elements to provide a unique, premium feel that sets the brand apart from local competitors.
*   **Functional Sections:**
    *   **Hero:** A high-impact entry point with a strong value proposition.
    *   **Industrial Credibility:** A dedicated strip for key performance stats (24/7 readiness, response times).
    *   **Operations & Services:** Organized into specialized categories with high-fidelity service cards.
    *   **Base Operations:** A clean, structured section for location details including the requested map container.
    *   **Initiate Dispatch:** A high-conversion contact form designed for priority requests.

The site is fully responsive and built with production-ready HTML/CSS/JS. If you'd like to adjust the color story, swap any of the industrial motifs, or refine the copy, just let me know!

## Brand & Style
The design system is built on the philosophy of "Industrial Luxe"—the intersection of heavy-duty capability and high-precision engineering. It targets high-end logistics and premium towing services, moving away from "emergency" cliches toward a "bespoke transport" narrative.

The visual style is **Corporate/Modern** with **Brutalist** undertones. It utilizes heavy strokes, grounded weight, and a structured layout to evoke reliability. The aesthetic response should be one of absolute confidence: the digital equivalent of a high-performance, polished heavy-duty machine. 

Key visual motifs include:
- **Diagonal Accents:** 45-degree angled section breaks to imply forward momentum.
- **Tread Texture:** Subtle micro-patterns on container backgrounds to mimic diamond-plate steel or tire treads.
- **Precision Framing:** Heavy 2px borders and strict grid alignment to emphasize order and control.

## Colors
The palette, titled "Asphalt & Hazard," avoids the saturated "caution" colors of competitors in favor of a more sophisticated, automotive-inspired range.

- **Primary (Amber-Orange):** Used exclusively for calls-to-action, status indicators, and critical highlights. It represents the "active" state of the machinery.
- **Secondary (Deep Charcoal):** The foundation. It provides a heavy, grounded surface that feels more premium than pure black.
- **Tertiary (Brushed Steel):** Used for primary typography and borders, providing a metallic, reflective quality against the dark background.
- **Neutral (Slate Gray):** Used for secondary containers and surface tiers to create depth without losing the industrial feel.

## Typography
Typography is used to reinforce the "Precision Engineering" theme. 

- **Headlines:** Archivo Narrow is utilized for its condensed, high-impact structure. Large-scale headlines should use tight tracking and be set in uppercase for a "stamped" metal appearance.
- **Body:** Inter provides a systematic, neutral contrast to the aggressive headlines, ensuring high legibility for technical service descriptions and logistics data.
- **Labels:** Geist (monospaced) is used for technical data, serial numbers, and metadata, giving the UI a "digital dashboard" feel.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fixed Grid** model on desktop (12 columns) to maintain a rigid, architectural structure. 

- **Gutter & Rhythm:** A strict 8px base unit drives all spacing. 24px gutters ensure that content blocks feel separated and distinct, like components on a chassis.
- **Desktop:** 12-column grid, max-width 1440px. Large margins (64px) create a gallery-like feel for vehicle photography.
- **Mobile:** 4-column fluid grid. Section padding is reduced, but the "weight" is maintained through heavy borders and consistent 20px edge margins.
- **Section Breaks:** Instead of standard horizontal lines, use 4px thick diagonal strokes at 45 degrees to divide major landing page sections.

## Elevation & Depth
In this design system, depth is achieved through **Tonal Layers** and **Low-Contrast Outlines** rather than soft shadows. Shadows should be used sparingly and only when they are "hard" (0-blur), mimicking industrial lighting.

- **Level 0 (Background):** Deep Charcoal (#1A1C1E).
- **Level 1 (Card/Container):** Slate Gray (#2C2E30) with a 1px solid Brushed Steel (#E2E2E2) border at 20% opacity.
- **Level 2 (Active/Floating):** Use a 2px solid primary Amber border to indicate the highest level of hierarchy.
- **Glass Effects:** Use a subtle backdrop blur on navigation bars (12px blur) to maintain a modern, "luxe" feel while keeping the heavy aesthetic.

## Shapes
The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px)**. Roundness is avoided to maintain the "Heavy Metal" and "Industrial" theme.

All buttons, input fields, cards, and image containers must have 90-degree corners. For specialized UI elements (like status badges), a "clipped corner" effect (chamfer) can be used at 45 degrees to reinforce the engineering motif.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid Amber (#F4A261) with black text, uppercase Archivo Narrow. Secondary buttons are "Ghost" style with a 2px Brushed Steel border.
- **Cards:** No shadows. Cards use a solid 1px border. For a "Premium" card variant, add a subtle 45-degree "hazard" stripe pattern in the top-right corner using a 10% opacity steel color.
- **Input Fields:** Bottom-border only or full-border with a technical "label-sm" positioned floating above. Backgrounds should be slightly darker than the surface container.
- **Status Chips:** High contrast. For "In Transit" or "Active," use the primary Amber. For "Delivered," use a muted steel blue. All chips are rectangular.
- **Imagery:** Photography should be desaturated (grayscale or high-contrast black and white) with primary Amber overlays on specific highlights (e.g., the tow hook, the company logo, or the vehicle's headlights).
- **Progress Indicators:** Use thick, blocky segments rather than smooth continuous bars to represent "loading" or "trip progress."
