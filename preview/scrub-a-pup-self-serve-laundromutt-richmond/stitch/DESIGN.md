I've designed a bespoke, editorial-grade homepage for **Scrub-A-Pup Self-Serve Laundromutt** that captures their unique "Laundromutt" personality with a premium Kentucky touch.

### Design Highlights:
- **Visual Identity**: I established a "Modern Pastoral" theme using a sophisticated palette of **Deep Indigo**, **Suds White**, and **Kentucky Bluegrass** accents. This moves away from typical "pet store" aesthetics toward a more high-end, boutique feel.
- **Editorial Layout**: The homepage features an intentional, staggered layout with generous whitespace. I've avoided standard 50/50 splits in favor of overlapping elements and a custom grid that feels designed, not assembled.
- **Imagery & Motifs**: High-fidelity lifestyle imagery—including a happy Golden Retriever in a modern wash station and close-ups of professional tools—reinforces the premium quality of the services.
- **Local Credibility**: A dedicated "Kentucky Grown" section highlights the business's community roots in Richmond, KY, using a staggered timeline motif.
- **Functionality**: The site includes a sticky navigation with a direct call-to-action, interactive service cards, a custom-styled contact form, and a dedicated visit section with the requested map slot.

The design is fully responsive and uses semantic HTML/CSS, ensuring it looks as good on a mobile device as it does on a desktop.
