I've completed the bespoke homepage for **Clipper City**, moving away from standard templates to create a high-end editorial experience.

### Design Highlights:
*   **Heritage & Steel Aesthetic**: I established a visual identity using **Heritage Navy** and **Raw Brass** accents, paired with high-contrast **Bodoni Moda** typography for an editorial feel.
*   **The Dossier (Services)**: Instead of a traditional grid, I designed a vertical "dossier" list for the services, emphasizing the craft and detail of each offering with clean lines and generous spacing.
*   **Asymmetrical Architecture**: The hero section and philosophy block utilize asymmetrical layouts to create a modern, premium rhythm that feels custom-built for a boutique Yakima brand.
*   **Production-Ready Details**: The site includes a dedicated map integration frame, a mobile-responsive navigation system, and a refined inquiry form, all maintaining the sharp, architectural style of the brand.

## Brand & Style

This design system embodies the intersection of traditional grooming heritage and contemporary editorial sophistication. Designed for a high-end barbershop experience, the aesthetic prioritizes authority, precision, and masculine elegance.

The visual style is **Minimalist-Architectural**. It rejects the softness of modern consumer apps in favor of sharp lines, high-contrast typography, and a "less but better" philosophy. The emotional response should be one of quiet confidence—evoking the atmosphere of a premium studio where craftsmanship is the primary focus. 

**Key Design Principles:**
- **Rigidity:** Every element is locked to a strict grid; no rounded corners are permitted.
- **Editorial Pace:** Use intentional whitespace (negative space) to allow high-quality photography to "breathe."
- **Materiality:** Use the 'Raw Brass' accent sparingly to denote luxury, similar to a physical brass inlay in wood or stone.
- **Asymmetry:** Break the visual rhythm with off-center text alignments and vertical rules to create a modern, curated feel.

## Colors

The palette is rooted in classic "Heritage Navy" and "Salt" to establish a clean, professional foundation. "Raw Brass" serves as the primary functional accent for calls to action and structural highlights.

- **Heritage Navy (#0F172A):** Used for primary text, backgrounds of immersive sections, and heavy structural elements.
- **Raw Brass (#B4975A):** Used for interactive states, thin borders, and decorative vertical rules.
- **Salt (#F8FAFC) & Slate (#E2E8F0):** Salt serves as the primary canvas color. Slate is used for subtle boundaries and secondary background fills.
- **Deep Slate (#1E293B):** Used for hover states on navy elements or for secondary UI components like footers.

## Typography

The typography leverages the tension between the classicism of **Bodoni Moda** and the industrial precision of **Inter**.

- **Headlines:** Set in Bodoni Moda. High-contrast strokes and elegant serifs provide the "Editorial" feel. Use `display-lg` for hero sections and impact statements.
- **Body:** Set in Inter. The neutral, sans-serif character ensures readability for service menus and descriptions.
- **Labels & Navigation:** Always use `label-caps`. This uppercase, tracked-out style mimics architectural signage and high-end fashion mastheads.
- **Alignment:** Experiment with "hanging" headlines where the text starts on a different grid column than the body copy to emphasize the asymmetrical motif.

## Layout & Spacing

The design system utilizes a **12-column fixed grid** for desktop and a **4-column fluid grid** for mobile. 

- **The Vertical Rule:** A signature motif. Use 1px vertical lines in `Raw Brass` or `Slate` to separate content columns or to lead the eye down the page.
- **Asymmetrical Balance:** Avoid centered layouts. Align content to the left or right 1/3rd of the grid to create dynamic visual interest.
- **Generous Gaps:** Section vertical spacing is intentionally large (128px+) to prevent the high-contrast elements from feeling cluttered.
- **Mobile Reflow:** On mobile, vertical rules should convert to horizontal dividers, and padding should reduce to 24px margins.

## Elevation & Depth

This design system is strictly **Flat-Architectural**. Depth is conveyed through layering and contrast rather than shadows or blurs.

- **Tonal Layering:** Use Navy surfaces against Salt backgrounds to create "islands" of content.
- **Zero Shadows:** Do not use box-shadows. Elevation is represented by 1px borders in a contrasting color (e.g., a Navy button on a Salt background).
- **Hard Edges:** All containers must have 0px border-radius.
- **Inlays:** For a sense of "physical" depth, use a 1px `Raw Brass` border nested inside a `Heritage Navy` container, creating a "frame" effect for images or featured content.

## Shapes

The shape language is defined by the **Right Angle**. 

- **Sharp Corners:** Every button, input field, card, and image container must have a 0px radius. This reinforces the precision and "sharpness" associated with premium barbering.
- **Geometric Accents:** Small squares or rectangles in `Raw Brass` can be used as bullet points or decorative ornaments at the intersection of grid lines.

## Components

### Buttons
- **Primary:** Heritage Navy background, Salt text, 0px radius. On hover, background shifts to Raw Brass.
- **Ghost:** Transparent background, 1px Heritage Navy border, Navy text.
- **Text:** Uppercase with wide letter spacing and a 1px Raw Brass underline that grows on hover.

### Cards & Photography
- **Product/Service Cards:** No background or shadow. Use 1px Slate borders. Images should be high-contrast (ideally B&W or desaturated) with a subtle grain.
- **The "Brass Frame":** Featured images can have a 1px Raw Brass border offset by 8px behind the image to create an asymmetrical "shadow" effect.

### Input Fields
- Underline-only style. A 1px Slate bottom border that turns to Raw Brass on focus. Labels are always `label-caps` positioned above the line.

### Chips & Tags
- Small rectangular blocks with Heritage Navy background and Salt `label-caps` text. No rounded corners.

### Lists
- Service menus should use a "dotted-leader" or a solid 1px Brass horizontal rule between the service name and the price, following the editorial style of a luxury menu.
